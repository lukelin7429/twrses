Unit 1: Tickets for the Big Show

⬇ PDF
Kenny

What are you up to this weekend?

Thomas

I was thinking about going to the concert in Taichung.

Kenny

Who is playing?

Thomas

Jolin is doing a show at the baseball park.

Kenny

Really? If she is coming, I would’ve thought it would be sold out by now.

Thomas

It is, but I have a friend who has tickets and can’t go.

Kenny

How many tickets does he have?

Thomas

He told me 4, but 2 are already spoken for.

Kenny

That leaves 2: 1 for you and 1 for me.

Thomas

Not so fast. I wanted to ask Colleen to come with me.

Kenny

You wouldn’t deny me the chance to see Jolin live, would you?

Thomas

I know you are a big fan, but Colleen is much better looking than you. Sorry.

Kenny

A big fan? I am her biggest fan. I have every album she has ever made and every DVD, too. It would crush me if I didn’t get to see her. You know I haven’t had the chance before, right? Thomas (sigh of pity): I know. I know. Ok, you can have the tickets and I’ll take Colleen to the movies instead. Kenny (tries to hug Thomas): Thank you. Thank you. Thank you. Thomas (defending against the hug): Alright! I got it. Just stop with the hugs. Jeez.

Kenny

Ok. How much are the tickets?

Thomas

My friend said they are 6000… Kenny (reaches for his wallet): No problem.

Thomas

Each! Kenny (stops taking his wallet out): On second thought, maybe I’ll just wait for the Live DVD. Thomas (shaking his head): Some fan you are.

課文朗讀（真人）
肯尼：你這個週末計畫做什麼呢？ 湯瑪斯：我想到台中聽演唱會。 肯尼：誰要演出呢？ 湯瑪斯：蔡依林要在棒球場做一場演出。 肯尼：真的嗎？如果她要來，我想現在入場券早就銷售一空了。 湯瑪斯：沒錯，我有一個朋友他有票，但是他沒辦法去。 肯尼：他有多少張票呢？ 湯瑪斯：他告訴我有4張票，但是已經有兩張被人訂走了， 肯尼：那還有兩張，一張給你，一張給我。 湯瑪斯：別這麼快就下結論，我是想邀請卡琳跟我一起去看。 肯尼：你不會拒絕給我這個機會去看蔡依林的現場演出吧？ 湯瑪斯：我知道你是他的忠心歌迷，但是卡琳比你好看多了。不好意思！ 肯尼：忠心的歌迷而已？我是她最忠心的歌迷，我有她曾經製作的每一張專輯和DVD。如果沒辦法去看她表演會讓我心碎的。你知道我以前從沒有這個機會的，不是嗎？ 湯瑪斯 (同情的嘆息)：我知道，我知道！好吧，票可以給你，而我帶卡琳去看電影好了。 肯尼 (試著要給湯馬斯一個擁抱)：謝謝！謝謝！謝謝你！ 湯瑪斯 (拒絕給他擁抱)：是的，我知道了！不要擁抱了，天哪！ 肯尼：好吧！那票要多少錢呢？ 湯瑪斯：我的朋友說要6000元。 肯尼 (伸手去拿他的皮夾)：沒問題！ 湯瑪斯：每一張票喔！ 肯尼 (拿皮夾的動作停下來)：在重新思考後，或許我應該等她現場演唱的DVD發行。 湯瑪斯 (搖頭)：你這個歌迷真是不怎麼樣！

閱讀理解 Questions

How did Thomas get a hold of the tickets?

He had a friend who had tickets, but couldn’t go.

Why did Kenny want to see the show so badly?

He wanted to see the show badly because he is Jolin’s biggest fan.

What changed Kenny’s mind about going?

The price of the tickets changed his mind about going.

Unit 2: The Shark’s Plight

⬇ PDF
Teacher

Ok class. Today we’re going to see how much you know about sharks.

Students

Cool. That sounds fun.

Teacher

First of all, what is the difference between a shark and all other fish?

Jack

I know! Sharks are bigger.

Teacher

Yes, some sharks are bigger, but some are smaller, too. Anyone else?

Frank

Sharks don’t have any bones.

Teacher

That is right, Frank. Sharks don’t have bones. Their skeletal structure is made entirely of cartilage just like your ear.

Thomas

And sharks are the only fish that kill people.

Teacher

That’s not true. Some fish kill people because they have poisonous spines. Really, though, sharks aren’t as dangerous as most people believe. Let me ask you this: How many people are attacked by sharks worldwide every year?

Jeffery

I think about 1, are attacked every year.

Teacher

Wow, that’s a lot and very specific, but it’s wrong. How about you, Sonia? What do you think?

Sonia

I think it is probably closer to 5000.

Teacher

Actually, on average, between 50 and 70 people are attacked by sharks.

Michelle

That’s not very many.

Teacher

No, it isn’t. Why do you think Sonia and Jeffery guessed such a high number?

Sam

Because people are very afraid of sharks and there are lots of documentaries on TV about shark attacks.

Teacher

That’s right. People have a strange relationship with sharks; they are afraid of them, but they love to watch TV shows about them. The reality is more people are killed by bees or struck by lightning every year, but movies like Jaws and other horror films have created a great fear of sharks in people. Of the people attacked, how many actually die, Tony?

Tony

Probably around 12.

Teacher

Yes, about 8 to 12 people die every year from shark attacks. What country reports the most shark attacks every year?

Michelle

Australia.

Teacher

Many people say that, but the USA is actually number 1. Most attacks are in Florida. Surprisingly, if you are attacked there, the chances of it being fatal are almost 0. This is because there is a shark “nursery” in the area, so most shark attacks in Florida involve inquisitive young sharks that aren’t big enough to cause much damage.

Henry

What kind of sharks are the most dangerous?

Alan

The Great White Shark.

Teacher

Yes, that is one. What are the other two?

Ann

The Whale Shark.

Teacher

That’s crazy! Whale sharks are one of the most passive creatures in the ocean. Plus, they don’t have teeth.

Tony

The tiger shark and the bull shark?

Teacher

Yes, they are the other 2. What makes the bull shark exceptionally different from most other sharks?

Alan

It can live for long periods of time in fresh water.

Teacher

Yes, Alan. How did you know that?

Alan

Learning about sharks is one of my hobbies.

Teacher

Good for you. That is a great hobby.

Alan

Did you know that bull sharks have been found hundreds of km’s upstream in many rivers?

Teacher

I didn’t know that. Thanks, Alan.

Bess

I heard that people kill many sharks every year just for their fins.

Teacher

Yes, but how many? Do you know?

Sonia

5000?

Teacher

Not even close.

Sam

5 million.

Teacher

Yes, if you multiply that number by 20. The answer is over 100, 000, 000 sharks are slaughtered every year.

Tim

That’s terrible. What can we do to help the sharks?

Teacher

Don’t buy shark meat, shark fin soup or any pills that contain shark byproducts.

Sonia

Why do people take shark pills?

Teacher

It’s because shark cartilage helps people with joint trouble. It is kind of like taking glucosamine.

Alan

Yeah. My grandpa takes them for arthritis. I told him about the shark killings, but he doesn’t care.

Teacher

Well…All we can do is to inform people and make them aware of the sharks’ plight. And pray!

課文朗讀（真人）
生物課的學生正在談論鯊魚及鯊魚的攻擊事件。 老師：好的，班上同學。今天我們要看你們對鯊魚的了解有多少。 學生：太棒了！聽起來蠻有趣的。 老師：首先，鯊魚和其他種類的魚差別在哪裡？ 傑克：我知道！鯊魚比較大。 老師：沒錯！有的鯊魚比較大，但也有的鯊魚比較小。其他人有答案嗎？ 法蘭克：鯊魚沒有任何的骨頭。 老師：是的！法蘭克。鯊魚沒有骨頭。他們的骨架結構全由軟骨構成的，就像你的耳朵一樣。 湯瑪士：鯊魚是唯一會殺人的魚。 老師：這不是真的。有一些魚會殺人，因為他們的刺是有毒的。然而說真的，鯊魚並沒有像大部分人們認為的那麼的危險。讓我來問你們這個問題：全世界每年有多少人被鯊魚攻擊？ 傑佛瑞：我認為每年約有1523人被攻擊。 老師：哇！這很多，而且數字很明確，但是這是錯誤的。你覺得怎樣？索尼婭， 妳認為呢？ 索尼婭：我想可能接近5000吧！ 老師：事實上，每年平均約有50到70的人被鯊魚攻擊過。 米雪兒：這不多啊！ 老師：對啊！不多。你認為為什麼索尼婭和傑佛瑞會猜那麼高的數字呢？ 山姆：因為大家都很怕鯊魚，而且有很多關於鯊魚攻擊的紀錄片在電視上播報。 老師：沒錯！人們跟鯊魚的關係很奇怪，他們害怕鯊魚，但又喜歡看鯊魚的電視節目。事實是這樣的，每年有更多人是被蜜蜂蜇死，或是被雷擊打死的。但像是大白鯊的電影及其它恐怖片已經使人們產生對於鯊魚產生很大的恐懼。而在被攻擊的人當中，真正有多少人喪生呢，湯尼？ 湯尼：大約有12個人左右。 老師：是的，每年大約8~12人死於鯊魚的攻擊。哪個國家每年有最多鯊魚攻擊事件的報告呢? 蜜雪兒：澳洲。 老師：很多人都會這樣子說，但事實上美國是第一名。大部分攻擊是在佛羅里達州。令人感到驚訝的是，假使你在那裡被攻擊，那死亡機率幾乎是零。 因為那裡是鯊魚養殖場，所以在佛羅里達州多數鯊魚攻擊事件都是與好奇的小鯊魚有關，由於牠們還太小，不至於造成很大的傷害。 亨利：最危險的是什麼鯊魚？ 艾倫：大白鯊。 老師：對，這是其中一種，還有另外兩種呢？ 安：鯨鯊老師：那太瘋狂了！鯨鯊是海洋上最溫馴的生物之一，此外，牠們並沒有牙齒。 湯尼：是虎鯊跟公牛鯊? 老師：是的，牠們就是另外的那兩種。公牛鯊特別和其它大多數的鯊魚不同的地方是甚麼呢? 艾倫：公牛鯊可以長時間在淡水中生活。 老師：沒錯，艾倫。你怎麼知道的？ 艾倫：學習鯊魚的相關知識是我的嗜好之一。 老師：很好，那是一個很棒的嗜好喔! 艾倫：你知道嗎，人們在許多河流發現公牛鯊逆游數百公里呢。 老師：我不知道，謝謝你，艾倫。 貝絲：我聽說人們為了鯊魚的鰭，每年殺死很多鯊魚。 老師：是的，但有多少？你知道嗎？ 蘇尼亞：5000嗎? 老師：差太多了。 山姆：5百萬。 老師：是的，如果你們把這數字乘上20，答案就是每年有超過1億的鯊魚被宰殺。 提姆：那太糟糕了，我們該如何幫助鯊魚？ 老師：不要買鯊魚肉、魚翅湯或任何有鯊魚副產品的藥丸。 蘇尼亞：為什麼人們要吃鯊魚藥丸? 老師：因為鯊魚軟骨有助於解決人們關節的問題，有點像是吃葡萄糖氨。 艾倫：沒錯。我祖父因為關節炎而服用它們。我告訴他關於屠殺鯊魚的事情， 但他並不在乎。 老師：嗯，我們所能做的只有為大家提供資訊，讓他們了解到鯊魚的困境，並且禱告。

閱讀理解 Questions

What do sharks have instead of bones?

They have cartilage.

What is shark cartilage used for?

It is used to make pills for various joint problems.

What can you do about it?

I can stop buying things made from or of sharks and inform people of the shark’s plight.

Unit 3: Endangered Sea Turtles

⬇ PDF
Darcy

What are you reading about, Chuck?

Chuck

I’m reading about Green Sea Turtles. It’s a kind of sea turtle.

Darcy

Oh. You mean like the Loggerhead Sea Turtle?

Chuck

Yes. Do you like sea turtles?

Darcy

I guess, but I don’t know much about them.

Chuck

I can teach you about them.

Darcy

Ok. Tell me about them.

Chuck

Did you know that many sea turtles are endangered?

Darcy

Actually, I did. I also heard that many different species used to lay their eggs on the beaches of Taiwan.

Chuck

Yes, but the green turtle is the only one that still does.

Darcy

Why is that?

Chuck

Well, before 1992 Taiwan didn’t do anything to protect their nesting areas and a lot of sites were lost because of urbanization.

Darcy

Do they protect them now?

Chuck

Yes, but only few spots on the surrounding islands of Taiwan remain.

Darcy

Is that the main reason their populations have declined.

Chuck

Really, it is much more horrifying than that. People catch them for their meat, their shells (carapaces) are used to make decorations, and some people even try to keep them as pets.

Darcy

My grandpa told me that some temples buy them and put them on display.

Chuck

That is strange considering that in Chinese culture they are a symbol of luck, prosperity and longevity.

Darcy

I know. You’d think people would want to protect them because of cultural importance.

Chuck

I guess the truth is that many people here are just not informed about what is going on so the poaching continues.

Darcy

Are other countries the same way?

Chuck

I suppose a lot of countries are, but some are being very proactive like Costa Rica for example. My cousin went there for a wedding in February and he said that the turtle nesting grounds were fenced off. They even had guards patrolling the area to make sure that no one gets in.

Darcy

It’s good to know that some places are taking action.

Chuck

It sure is and hopefully our country and others will start doing more as well.

Darcy

I’m going to go home and look on the internet. Maybe there are some volunteer jobs available so that I can do my part.

Chuck

That’s a great idea. Let me know if you find anything and I will sign up, too.

Darcy

Great! I’ll catch ya later.

Chuck

Alright. Later, man.

課文朗讀（真人）
達西：達西你在讀什麼書？ 查克：我正在閱讀有關於綠蠵龜的書，這是一種海龜。 達西：喔！你的意思是說像是蠵龜的海龜嗎？ 查克：是的，你喜歡海龜嗎？ 達西：我想是吧！但是我對牠們並沒有很多的了解。 查克：我可以敎你相關知識。 達西：好！你就告訴我吧。 查克：你知道很多的海龜已經瀕臨絕種了嗎？ 達西：事實上我知道。我也聽說很多不同種類的海龜以前在台灣的海灘下蛋。 查克：是的，但是現在只剩下綠蠵龜還會在這裡下蛋。 達西：為什麼會這樣呢？ 查克：嗯，在1992年之前，台灣並沒有任何作為來保護牠們築巢的區域，而且很多的築巢區都因為都市化而不見了。 達西：現在有保護牠們嗎？ 查克：是的，但是只有在台灣離島的一些地點還保留。 達西：這就是牠們的數量會下降的主要原因。 查克：事實比這個更恐怖。人們抓海龜來吃牠們的肉，或是用牠們的殼來做裝飾品，而甚至有些人把牠們當寵物養。 達西：我的祖父告訴我，有一些廟宇買海龜，並且拿牠們來展示。 查克：這就奇怪了，在中國文化中牠們是好運、繁榮、長壽的象徵。 達西：我知道，你會認為人們會想要保護海龜，因為牠們在文化上的重要性。 查克：我想事實上是這樣的，這邊很多人對海龜的現況並不知情，所以盜獵的行為仍然持續中。 達西：其他的國家也相同嗎？ 查克：我想很多的國家也是這樣的，但是有些像哥斯大黎加的國家就預先採取行動來保護牠們。我的表弟二月份到那裡舉行一場婚禮，他說：在烏龜的築巢地都被用籬笆圍起來。他們甚至還有警衛巡邏這個區域，以確保沒有人會進來盜獵。 達西：知道有些地方有採取行動真好。 查克：的確是如此。但願我們的國家和其他國家也能多做一點事來保護海龜。 達西：我想要回家搜尋網路資訊，或許可以找到一些義工的工作，這樣我便可以盡一點心意。 查克：這是很棒的點子。如果你有找到任何義工的工作，要我知道，我也要報名。 達西：太棒了！等會兒見。 查克：好的老兄，待會兒見。

閱讀理解 Questions

Are there lots of Green Sea Turtles left?

No, they are endangered.

What is one of the main reasons that they are endangered?

One of the main reasons is the loss of nesting sights due to human encroachment.

What has Costa Rica done to help protect sea turtles?

They have turned the turtles’ nesting sites into environmental sanctuaries.

Unit 4: Massive Earthquake Strikes Japan

⬇ PDF
Jean

Did you hear about the catastrophe in Japan?

Tina

No, I didn’t. What happened?

Jean

There was a huge earthquake just off the coast. It measured (registered) 9 on the Richter scale.

Tina

9? That is the largest one I’ve ever heard of.

Jean

But that’s not the worst part.

Tina

Really? What else happened?

Jean

Because the earthquake was offshore, it caused a tsunami. The following swell went as far as 4 km’s inland some places. It capsized boats and some floated down streets. It washed away buildings, farms, and even entire villages. It was like a giant bulldozer leveled the place.

Tina

Are you serious?

Jean

The footage has been all over the news. I can’t believe you haven’t seen or heard of this yet.

Tina

Was there much damage?

Jean

帕特慢慢的走進他的辦公室，他笨重地走到桌子前，然後猛然坐到位置上。他的眼睛有很大的黑眼圈，看起來像是根本沒睡覺的樣子。他的同事喬注意到帕特似乎看起來不太好，便走到他的辦公室隔間跟他說話。 喬：哎呀，瞧！是甚麼人來了，怎麼了？ 帕特：我好累！我昨晚一晚沒有睡。 喬：為什麼沒睡，你又喝太多綠茶了嗎？ 帕特：不是！我的臥室裡有一隻蚊子。 喬：天哪！我很討厭發生這種事。他們真是害蟲。 帕特：那隻愚蠢的蚊子就是不肯放過我，牠不斷試著要停在我的肩膀想叮我。 喬：有叮到嗎？ 帕特：一定有，我今天早上醒來時，我脖子的後面有一個小腫包，而且很癢。 喬：這真是脖子上的痛，不是嗎？(註：此句引申為令人討厭的事，他用這句話來消遣帕特。) 帕特：很好笑，整天抓脖子的人可不是你啊！ 喬：事實上，我不是那麼在乎蚊子叮，是蚊子在耳邊嗡嗡作響的聲音讓我快要瘋掉。你打蚊子，牠們就離開一下下，但是總是會再飛回來。 帕特：而且不管何時開燈要抓蚊子，牠們就會突然消失。 喬：牠們真是鬼鬼祟祟的小害蟲。 帕特：不要忘記加上「令人生氣」這個形容詞。 喬：我想這就是為什麼人們稱蚊子為小蟲的原因了。

Unit 9: The Dodge ball Cheater

⬇ PDF
James

It was great. We got to play dodge ball in PE.

JF

Great! I know how much you love dodge ball.

James

I sure do.

JF

Did your team win?

James

We should have.

JF

What do you mean?

James

Well, it was down to 3 against 1; 3 for us and 1 for them.

JF

So why didn’t you win?

James

I hit the other player in the leg, but the ref didn’t see it. And the player said I was lying.

JF

So he should have been out.

James

I know, but he wasn’t. Then he hit one of our guys with the ball, so we lost a player and it was down to 2 on 1.

JF

His odds got better, but you still had an advantage.

James

Then I threw the ball at him and it grazed his shoulder, but he said it didn’t hit him again!

JF

And, I suppose, he took out another one of your teammates.

James

That is exactly what happened. Now it was 1 on 1 and I was off my game. I more or less gave up and he won.

JF

You have to remember that not everyone is honest and plays by the rules. Don’t let them get under your skin.

James

But it made me so angry. He had to cheat to win.

JF

That’s how guys like him win. They get you upset, unfocussed and make you easier to beat.

James

Maybe I should have used my emotions against him.

JF

Yes, next time, don’t let him get the best of you. Just stay on your game and take him out.

James

Anyway, I would rather lose honestly than win by cheating.

JF

That’s my boy! And like they say: Cheaters never prosper.

James

But he won!?

JF

Maybe they mean in the long run.

課文朗讀（真人）
詹姆斯剛從學校回到家，他的父親在門前跟他打招呼。 詹姆斯的爸爸：兒子，今天上學還好嗎？ 詹姆斯：很棒啊！我們在體育課打躲避球。 詹姆斯的爸爸：很好啊！我知道你有多麼喜歡打躲避球。 詹姆斯：當然！ 詹姆斯的爸爸：你們那一隊贏了嗎？ 詹姆斯：我們應該是要贏的。 詹姆斯的爸爸：這句話是什麼意思？ 詹姆斯：嗯，比賽到最後剩下3對1；我們剩下3個，對方只有一個。 詹姆斯的爸爸：這樣子怎麼還輸了呢？ 詹姆斯：我打到剩下那一個人的腳，但是裁判沒看到。然後那個人說我在說謊。 詹姆斯的爸爸：所以他應該是要出去的。 詹姆斯：我知道，但是他沒有。然後他用球打中我們其中一個人，所以我們就損失一名選手，比數變成2比1。 詹姆斯的爸爸：他的成功機率變高了，但是你們仍佔優勢。 詹姆斯：接著我用球打他，並且擦過他的肩膀。但是他又說：球並沒有打中他。 詹姆斯的爸爸：我想他可能又把你們另外一個隊友打出去了。 詹姆斯：沒錯！事情就是這樣。現在是1比1，而且我已經無心於比賽了。我多少有一些放棄了，結果他贏了。 詹姆斯的爸爸：你一定要記得，不是每一個人都很誠實，並且遵守比賽規則，千萬不要讓他們把你惹毛了。 詹姆斯：但這件事真的讓我很生氣，他非得靠作弊來贏球。 詹姆斯的爸爸：這就是像他那種人贏球的方式，他們惹你生氣，使你無法集中精神，讓你更容易被擊敗。 詹姆斯：也許我應該把我的負面情緒拿來對抗他。 詹姆斯的爸爸：是的，下一次不要讓他得逞，專注於你的比賽，並且擊敗他。 詹姆斯：不管怎樣，我寧願輸的很誠實，也不願靠作弊來贏球。 詹姆斯的爸爸：這才是我的乖孩子，就如同人們說的：作弊的人是不會成功的。 詹姆斯：但是他贏了啊！ 詹姆斯的爸爸：也許他們是指這種人最終還是會失敗的。

Unit 10: Funny Fishing Bait

⬇ PDF
Ryan

How about this spot? It looks like a good place to catch some fish.

Vernon

Sure. I’m tired of walking around on these big rocks anyway. The boys set down their backpacks and take out their fishing rods. They tie hooks on the end of their fishing lines. Now they are ready to put bait on their hooks.

Ryan

What are you using for bait, Vernon? I’m using an earthworm. I’ve always had good luck using them.

Vernon

Really! I’ve never had any luck with earthworms. I usually use these. Vernon pulls a great big spider out of a container in his backpack. The creepy looking thing is almost as big as his hand. Ryan jumps back in fright.

Ryan

Holy cow! Are you trying to catch a fish or scare it to death?

Vernon

Just be quiet. You try it your way and I’ll try it mine. The boys cast their hooks into the water. Immediately, Ryan feels something pulling on his line.

Ryan

I got one! I got one!

Vernon

Quick! Reel it in. Ryan reels as fast as he can. Then Vernon starts shouting.

Vernon

I caught something, too. WOOHOO!

Ryan

The fishing is really good today, hey buddy?

Vernon

Indeed! We’ll be eating like kings tonight. After reeling for a bit, Vernon sees the end of his line.

Vernon

I caught something alright, but you’re not going to be happy.

Ryan

What do you mean?

Vernon

I think my spider caught your earthworm.

Ryan

You must be joking. Both of them pull their tangled lines from the water. They can clearly see the spider holding the worm tightly with its front legs and biting it.

Ryan

I’m never going fishing with you and your gigantic spider again.

Vernon

Come on. He was just hungry. Next time, I will feed him first.

課文朗讀（真人）
有兩位十幾歲的年輕人沿著一條在南投的溪流散步，這兩個男孩想要尋找一個釣魚的地方。他們沿著很多岩石的河岸健行，繞過了一兩個河灣，直到他們走到一個看起來還蠻適合釣魚的地方。 里安：這個地點如何？看起來像是可以釣到一些魚的好地方。 弗農：好啊，一直在大岩石上面走路我也感到很厭煩了。 這兩個男孩把他們的背包放下，並且拿出他們的釣竿。他們把魚鉤綁在釣魚線的一端，現在，他們準備把魚餌放在魚鉤上。 里安：弗農，你要用什麼來當魚餌呢？我要用蚯蚓，我用蚯蚓的運氣總是不錯。 弗農：真的，我用蚯蚓的運氣都很不好，通常我都是用這種魚餌的。 弗農從他背包裡的容器拿出一隻很大的蜘蛛，這隻看起來令人毛骨悚然的蜘蛛跟他的手幾乎一樣大。里安嚇得往後跳。 里安：天哪！你是要來抓魚，還是要把魚嚇死的呢？ 弗農：安靜點，你用你的方式，我用我的方法。 這兩個男孩把他們的魚鉤投入水中，里安立刻就覺得有東西在拉他的線。 里安：我釣到一隻了，我釣到一隻了！ 弗農：趕快捲上來。 里安盡快地收線，而緊接著弗農開始大叫。 弗農：我也釣到東西了，呦呼！ 里安：嗨！夥伴，今天釣魚的狀況很不錯呢？ 弗農：的確如此，今天晚上我們會像國王一樣享用大餐。 在捲了一陣子之後，弗農看到他線的末端。 弗農：我是釣到東西沒錯，但是你一定不會很高興。 里安：什麼意思？ 弗農：我想我的蜘蛛抓到你的蚯蚓了。 里安：你一定是在開玩笑吧！ 兩個男孩把糾纏在一起的線從水中拉出來，他們可以很清楚的看到蜘蛛用前腳緊緊抓住蚯蚓，而且還在咬牠。 里安：我以後再也不要跟你以及你那隻大蜘蛛來釣魚了。 弗農：拜託！牠只是肚子餓，下一次我一定先餵飽牠。
