Unit 1: The Androgynous New Student

⬇ PDF
Tyler

Hey, you’re new around here, aren’t you?

嘿，你是這裡的新同學，對吧？
NS

Yeah, it’s my first week at this school. Tyler is a bit attracted to the feminine beauty of the new student and becomes bold.

對啊，這是我在這所學校的第一週。泰勒有點被新同學的女性美吸引，變得大膽起來。
Tyler

Listen, there is a school dance this weekend and it would be a good place to meet new people. And seeing I don’t have a date, well, maybe you’d like to go with me?

聽著，這個週末學校有一場舞會，那會是認識新朋友的好地方。而且我剛好沒有約會對象，那個……你要不要跟我一起去？
NS

(chuckling) I’m sorry. I’m not gay. Tyler suddenly realizes that the new student is not a girl. He is utterly embarrassed.

（輕笑）不好意思，我不是同性戀。泰勒突然意識到這位新同學不是女生，他感到非常尷尬。
Tyler

Uh! Sorry. Wow, talk about sticking your foot in your mouth. Boy is my face red. NS (laughing wildly): You thought I was a girl! I don’t know whether to laugh or cry. They both laugh at the humorous misunderstanding.

呃！抱歉。哇，真是說錯話說到家了。我的臉肯定紅透了。新同學（狂笑）：你以為我是女生耶！我都不知道該笑還是該哭了。他們兩人都被這個好笑的誤會逗笑了。
NS

By the way, my name is Pat. And yes I realize the irony. Tyler (trying desperately to not to laugh): You have to be kidding. Sorry, but that is too funny. Anyway, it is nice to meet you “Pat”. It is a strange start to a new friendship.

順帶一提，我的名字是 Pat。是的，我知道這很諷刺。泰勒（拚命忍住不笑）：你該不會是在開玩笑吧。抱歉，但這真的太好笑了。不管怎樣，很高興認識你，「Pat」。這是一段新友誼的奇特開始。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

How long has the new student been going to this school?

It is his first week at this school.

Why does Tyler mistake the new student for a girl?

He mistakes him for a girl because the new student is looks feminine.

Why is it ironic that the new student’s name is Pat?

It is ironic because Pat can be a girl’s or a boy’s name.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

androgynous(adj.)雌雄同體的With her short hair and plain clothes, the model had an androgynous look.這位模特兒留著短髮、穿著樸素的衣服，看起來雌雄同體。meander(v.)閒逛We decided to meander through the old town with no particular plan.我們決定在舊城區隨意閒逛，沒有特定計畫。hallway(n.)走廊The students gathered in the hallway before class started.學生們在上課前聚集在走廊上。strike up(ph.)開始(交談)He decided to strike up a conversation with the girl next to him.他決定和坐在旁邊的女孩開始交談。attract(v.)吸引Her friendly smile tends to attract new friends easily.她友善的笑容很容易吸引新朋友。feminine(adj.)女性的She wore a soft, feminine dress to the party.她穿了一件柔美的女性化洋裝去參加派對。bold(adj.)大膽的It was a bold move for him to ask her out on the first day.他第一天就約她出去，是個大膽的舉動。date(n.)約會對象I still have not found a date for the school dance.我還沒找到校園舞會的約會對象。chuckle(v.)咯咯地笑She began to chuckle when she heard his silly joke.聽到他的傻笑話，她咯咯地笑了起來。utterly(adv.)十足地He felt utterly embarrassed after making the mistake.他犯了那個錯誤之後感到十足地尷尬。stick one’s foot in one’s mouth(ph.)說錯了話；說了不該說的話I really stuck my foot in my mouth when I mentioned her ex-boyfriend.我提到她前男友的時候，真的是說錯了話。misunderstanding(n.)誤解The whole argument was caused by a simple misunderstanding.整場爭吵都是因為一個單純的誤解引起的。irony(n.)諷刺There is a certain irony in a name that fits both boys and girls.一個男女皆適用的名字，帶有某種諷刺意味。desperately(adv.)極度地He was desperately trying not to laugh out loud in class.他極度地努力忍住不在課堂上笑出聲。humorous(adj.)幽默的；滑稽的They both laughed at the humorous misunderstanding between them.他們兩人都因彼此之間這個滑稽的誤會而大笑。

完整教學音檔

📖 課文教學（中文講解）

小測驗 Quick Check

1. What event does Tyler mention when talking to the new student?

2. How does Tyler react right after he realizes the new student is not a girl?

3. What does Tyler try hard not to do when he learns the new student's name is Pat?

Unit 2: Summer’s Over

⬇ PDF
David

Oh my god, the summer is almost over.

天啊，暑假快要結束了。
Frank

Yeah, I know. I was just getting used to sitting on my butt doing absolutely nothing and now it’s time to head back to school.

對啊，我知道。我才剛習慣整天坐著什麼事都不做，現在就要回學校了。
David

How am I ever going to get up in the morning? I am so used to sleeping in.

我到底要怎麼在早上起床啊？我已經很習慣睡到很晚了。
Frank

Tell me about it! I’ve been sleeping till noon every day.

別提了！我最近每天都睡到中午。
David

I guess there won’t be any more late nights playing video games and watching cartoons.

我猜以後不會再有熬夜打電動、看卡通的日子了。
Frank

I’m sure there will be plenty of late nights doing homework, though.

不過我相信一定會有很多熬夜寫作業的夜晚。
David

You got that right. Argh!

你說得對。啊！
Frank

At least we will get to see some of our friends.

至少我們還能見到一些朋友。
David

I guess that’s true, but at what cost? We will be too busy to talk to them.

我想是沒錯，但代價是什麼呢？我們會忙到沒時間跟他們說話。
Frank

Maybe, we should just quit and get a job.

也許我們應該乾脆放棄，去找工作。
David

But we are only ten. Who would hire us?

但我們才十歲，誰會雇用我們啊？
Frank

I suppose you’re right. Maybe, when we’re older we can quit.

我想你說得對。也許等我們長大一點就可以不用讀書了。
David

Sure, like 15. That is really old!

對啊，像十五歲。那真的很老了！
Frank

Yeah, those guys have it made.

是啊，那些傢伙真好命。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

Why is David worried about getting up in the morning for school?

He is worried because he is used to sleeping in.

How late has Frank been sleeping every day?

He has been sleeping till noon every day.

Why does Frank think that there will still be lots of late nights when school starts?

He thinks there will be lots of late because they will be doing homework.

Why does David think quitting school to get a job is a bad idea?

He thinks it is a bad idea because nobody would hire them.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

get used to(ph.)習慣於It took me a while to get used to waking up early.我花了一段時間才習慣早起。butt(n.)屁股He sat on his butt all afternoon watching television.他整個下午都坐著看電視。absolutely(adv.)完全地I am absolutely sure that she will come to the party.我完全確定她會來參加派對。head(v.)出發We should head home before it gets dark.我們應該在天黑之前出發回家。plenty of(ph.)很多There is plenty of food left on the table.桌上還剩下很多食物。sleep in(ph.)睡懶覺；晚起On weekends, I like to sleep in until ten in the morning.週末的時候，我喜歡睡懶覺到十點。till(prep.)直到She studied till midnight to prepare for the test.她讀書讀到半夜以準備考試。cartoon(n.)卡通My little brother watches cartoons every Saturday morning.我弟弟每個星期六早上都會看卡通。homework(n.)家庭作業I still have a lot of homework to finish tonight.我今晚還有很多家庭作業要完成。at least(ph.)至少We should study for at least one hour every day.我們每天至少應該讀書一小時。at what cost(ph.)但代價是什麼；付出什麼代價He got the promotion, but at what cost to his health.他得到了升遷，但這對他的健康付出了什麼代價呢。quit(v.)放棄；辭去She decided to quit her job to travel around the world.她決定辭去工作去環遊世界。hire(v.)雇用The restaurant plans to hire two more waiters this month.這家餐廳這個月計畫再雇用兩名服務生。have it made(ph.)生活優渥；一切順遂With a big house and no worries, they really have it made.有一棟大房子又無憂無慮，他們的生活真是優渥。get up(ph.)起床I have to get up at six every school day.我上學的日子每天都必須六點起床。

完整教學音檔

📖 課文教學（中文講解）

小測驗 Quick Check

1. What were David and Frank doing all summer, according to the dialogue?

2. What does Frank say they will not be able to do once school starts?

3. At what age do David and Frank joke that people can quit school and get a job?

Unit 3: Asking For Assistance

⬇ PDF
Evan

Excuse me.

不好意思。
Clerk

Yes, can I help you?

是的，我能幫你什麼忙嗎？
Evan

I’m sorry to bother you, but I have a question I was hoping you could help me with.

很抱歉打擾你，但我有個問題想請你幫忙。
Clerk

No problem! Fire away.

沒問題！請說。
Evan

Where are the PSP games?

請問 PSP 遊戲在哪裡？
Clerk

They are in the electronics department on the aisle 4 next to the kitchen appliances. Evan leaves briefly, but returns.

它們在電子產品區的第4走道，就在廚房器具旁邊。艾文暫時離開，隨後又回來了。
Evan

I really hate to be a nuisance, but I’d like to see one of the games and it’s locked inside a display case.

我真的很不想麻煩你，但我想看看其中一款遊戲，可是它被鎖在展示櫃裡。
Clerk

No worries! I’ll open it for you. The sales associate leads Evan back to the electronics to open the cabinet.

別擔心！我幫你打開。銷售人員帶艾文回到電子產品區去打開櫃子。
Evan

Thanks a lot. You’ve been a real help.

太謝謝你了，你真的幫了我一個大忙。
Clerk

That’s what I’m here for.

這是我的職責所在。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

What department are the PSP games in?

They are in the electronics department.

Why can’t Evan take a look at the game he likes?

He can’t take a look at it because it is locked inside a display case.

Is the clerk bothered by Evan’s questions?

No, he isn’t. He seems very helpful.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

assistance(n.)幫助The lost tourist asked a police officer for assistance.迷路的觀光客向警察尋求幫助。bother(v.)打擾I am sorry to bother you, but do you have a moment?很抱歉打擾你，你現在方便嗎？fire away(ph.)(讓人)開始說If you have any questions, go ahead and fire away.如果你有任何問題，儘管開口問吧。electronics(n.)電子器件The store sells cameras and other electronics.這家店販售相機和其他電子器件。aisle(n.)走道You can find the snacks in the third aisle.你可以在第三條走道找到零食。appliance(n.)器具A refrigerator is a common kitchen appliance.冰箱是常見的廚房器具。nuisance(n.)討厭的人(或事物)The constant noise from the road is a real nuisance.馬路上不斷傳來的噪音真的很惱人。display case(ph.)展示櫃The jewelry was locked inside a glass display case.珠寶被鎖在玻璃展示櫃裡。sales associate(ph.)銷售人員A friendly sales associate helped me find the right size.一位親切的銷售人員幫我找到合適的尺寸。cabinet(n.)櫃子She keeps the dishes in the kitchen cabinet.她把碗盤放在廚房的櫃子裡。no problem(ph.)沒問題Can you help me carry this box? Sure, no problem.你可以幫我搬這個箱子嗎？當然，沒問題。briefly(adv.)短暫地The clerk stepped away briefly to check the stock room.店員短暫離開去查看庫房。no worries(ph.)不用擔心；別在意I am sorry I am late. No worries, we just got here too.抱歉我遲到了。不用擔心，我們也才剛到。lead(v.)帶領The guide will lead us through the museum.導覽員將帶領我們參觀博物館。real(adj.)真正的；十足的Thank you for staying late, you have been a real help.謝謝你留下來幫忙，你真的幫了大忙。

完整教學音檔

📖 課文教學（中文講解）

小測驗 Quick Check

1. What does Evan want to see that is locked inside a display case?

2. What is located next to the kitchen appliances in the store?

3. What does the sales associate do after Evan explains his problem?

Unit 4: A Valentine’s Vacation Gone Wrong

⬇ PDF
Fred

Where are we going to have dinner tonight?

我們今晚要去哪裡吃晚餐？
June

How about you pick the restaurant?

不如你來選餐廳吧？
Fred

What about a Japanese restaurant?

吃日本料理怎麼樣？
June

We just had Japanese food last Saturday.

我們上星期六才剛吃過日本料理。
Fred

Let’s go to an all-you-can-eat place then.

那我們去吃到飽的地方吧。
June

You know I don’t like buffets.

你知道我不喜歡自助餐。
Fred

I know! Korean.

我知道了！韓式料理。
June

I don’t feel like Korean today. Fred (growing frustrated): Italian?

我今天不想吃韓式料理。弗雷德（漸漸感到挫折）：義大利菜呢？
June

We can have Italian any time. Fred (angrily): Then how about instant noodles at 7-11.

義大利菜我們隨時都可以吃。弗雷德（生氣地）：那不然去7-11吃泡麵怎麼樣。
June

Why? What’s your problem?

為什麼？你有什麼問題？
Fred

You told me to choose a restaurant, but you are being fussy and shooting down all of my suggestions. Why don’t you just pick it yourself? June (pouting): Then let’s just go back to the hotel and go to bed. Fred (rolling his eyes): Here we go!

你叫我選一家餐廳，但你卻很挑剔，把我提的建議全部否決。你為什麼不自己選？準恩（噘著嘴）：那我們乾脆回飯店睡覺算了。弗雷德（翻白眼）：又來了！
June

What is that supposed to mean?

那句話是什麼意思？
Fred

Nothing. Nothing.

沒事，沒事。
June

You are ruining my Valentine’s Day.

你正在毀掉我的情人節。
Fred

Me? That’s right. It’s always my fault.

我？沒錯，永遠都是我的錯。
June

If you would just pick a restaurant that I LIKED…

如果你能選一家我喜歡的餐廳……
Fred

What am I, a mind reader or something? Why can’t you just tell me?

我是什麼，讀心術者嗎？你為什麼不能直接告訴我？
June

You don’t love me. Fred (sarcastically): Right. I don’t love you because I can’t read your mind.

你根本不愛我。弗雷德（諷刺地）：對啊，我不愛你，因為我不會讀心術。
June

If you loved me, you would know what I like.

如果你愛我，你就會知道我喜歡什麼。
Fred

I know one thing you like.

我知道一件你喜歡的事。
June

What’s that?

是什麼？
Fred

Arguing.

吵架。
June

You are so childish. I hate you! June stomps back to the car as fast as she can. She slams the door. Fred hears the locks on the car door click. He shakes his head and mutters to himself…

你真幼稚，我討厭你！準恩盡快跺著腳走回車上，用力甩上車門。弗雷德聽到車門上鎖的喀嚓聲，搖搖頭，自言自語地嘀咕著……
Fred

It’s going to be a long night.

看來今晚會很漫長。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

Why doesn’t June want Japanese food?

She doesn’t want it because they just had it recently.

Why does Fred get angry?

He gets angry because June keeps saying no to all of his suggestions and is being fussy.

What does June conclude when Fred can’t choose a restaurant she likes?

She concludes that Fred doesn’t love her.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

all-you-can-eat(adj.)吃到飽的We went to an all-you-can-eat barbecue restaurant for my birthday.我們去了一家吃到飽的烤肉餐廳慶祝我的生日。buffet(n.)自助餐The hotel offers a big breakfast buffet every morning.這間飯店每天早上都提供豐盛的自助早餐。Korean(adj.)韓國的She loves eating spicy Korean food on weekends.她喜歡在週末吃辣的韓國菜。Italian(adj.)義大利的We ordered a large Italian pizza for the party.我們為派對訂了一個大的義大利披薩。fussy(adj.)挑剔的My little brother is very fussy about what he eats.我弟弟對吃的東西非常挑剔。shoot down(ph.)否決The manager decided to shoot down every idea we suggested.經理否決了我們提出的每一個點子。pout(v.)噘嘴The little girl began to pout when she did not get the toy.小女孩沒拿到玩具時開始噘嘴。be supposed to(ph.)應該You are supposed to finish your homework before dinner.你應該在晚餐前完成你的功課。ruin(v.)毀壞The heavy rain almost ruined our picnic plans.大雨幾乎毀了我們的野餐計畫。sarcastically(adv.)諷刺地He replied sarcastically when his friend asked a silly question.當朋友問了一個蠢問題時，他諷刺地回答。argue(v.)爭吵The two brothers often argue about small things.這兩兄弟常常為了小事爭吵。stomp(v.)跺腳He began to stomp his feet when he did not get his way.他沒有得到他想要的結果時開始跺腳。slam(v.)猛地關上She was so angry that she slammed the door on her way out.她氣得離開時把門猛地關上。mutter(v.)低聲嘀咕He began to mutter to himself after losing the game.輸掉比賽後，他開始自言自語地低聲嘀咕。frustrated(adj.)沮喪的；感到挫折的Fred felt frustrated because June kept rejecting every restaurant he suggested.弗雷德感到很挫折，因為準恩一直拒絕他提議的每一家餐廳。

完整教學音檔

📖 課文教學（中文講解）

小測驗 Quick Check

1. What does Fred suggest eating after June rejects Italian food?

2. What does June do right after Fred rolls his eyes and says "Here we go"?

3. What does June do at the very end of the conversation?

Unit 5: Something in the Woods

⬇ PDF
Sally

Did you hear that?

你聽到那個聲音了嗎？
Tina

Yeah. What do you think it is?

有啊。你覺得那是什麼？
Harriet

I don’t know. It sounded like a pig.

我不知道。聽起來像一頭豬。
Sally

A pig? It sounded more like a ghost to me.

一頭豬？我聽起來比較像鬼。
Tina

A pig! A ghost! You are both crazy. I think it is a wolf.

豬！鬼！你們兩個都瘋了。我覺得那是一隻狼。
Harriet

We are in Taiwan. There aren’t any wolves here.

我們在台灣耶。這裡才沒有狼。
Sally

Should we go and check it out?

我們該過去看看嗎？
Tina

Ok, but Harriet has to go first.

好啊，但是哈莉葉要先走。
Harriet

Why me?

為什麼是我？
Sally

Because you are the fastest.

因為你跑得最快。
Harriet

No, I’m not. I’m the slowest. That’s why you want me to go first.

不，我才不是。我跑得最慢。這就是為什麼你們要我先走。
Tina

What’s your point?

你的重點是什麼？
Harriet

Whatever is hiding in the bushes will catch me first and you both can get away safely while it eats me.

不管是什麼躲在灌木叢裡，牠會先抓到我，然後你們兩個就能趁牠吃掉我的時候安全逃走。
Sally

It’s like the old saying, “I don’t have to outrun the bear; I just have to outrun you.”

這就像那句老話說的：「我不需要跑得比熊快，我只需要跑得比你快。」
Harriet

Very funny. You’re a real comedian. Suddenly a little white ball of fur streaks across the campsite and disappears into the bushes on the other side. Unit 9: Thinking about Egypt

⬇ PDF
Jared

So, I was thinking about taking a trip this week?

所以，我在想這禮拜要不要去旅行？
Kent

Where are you thinking about going?

你在考慮去哪裡呢？
Jared

I haven’t made up my mind yet. Do you have any suggestions?

我還沒決定，你有什麼建議嗎？
Kent

Well… It is winter, so you should go south where it will be warmer. How about Kenting?

嗯……現在是冬天，你應該往南邊走，那裡比較溫暖。墾丁怎麼樣？
Jared

Actually, I was thinking about something overseas;3 Maybe Egypt.

其實我在想要不要去海外；也許去埃及。
Kent

That’s kind of far for a spur-of-the-moment vacation.

臨時起意的假期去那麼遠有點誇張。
Jared

I know, but I’ve always wanted to see the pyramids.

我知道，但我一直很想看金字塔。
Kent

Me, too. They are so amazing and mysterious.

我也是。它們既壯觀又神秘。
Jared

And I want to see a mummy, too.

而且我也想看看木乃伊。
Kent

Not me. Mummies scare me. I find them creepy.

我才不要。木乃伊讓我很害怕，我覺得很毛骨悚然。
Jared

What about the Sphinx?

那人面獅身像呢？
Kent

I wouldn’t mind seeing that. It would be awesome to actually say that I’ve stood next to something so ancient.

我倒是不介意看看那個。能親口說自己站在如此古老的東西旁邊，一定很棒。
Jared

I guess that I have made up my mind then. Egypt it is.

我想我已經決定了，就去埃及吧。
Kent

Great! Can I come along?

太好了！我可以一起去嗎？
Jared

Sorry, but you would cramp my style.

抱歉，但你會妨礙到我。
Kent

What? Cramp YOUR style? That’s no way to talk to a friend.

什麼？妨礙到你？這樣跟朋友說話不太好吧。
Jared

But what if I met a nice girl and wanted to spend time with her? You would be like a third wheel.

但如果我遇到一個不錯的女生，想跟她多相處呢？你就會像個電燈泡一樣。
Kent

You’re just going to Egypt to pick up girls, aren’t you?

你去埃及該不會就只是為了把妹吧？
Jared

I wouldn’t say JUST to pick up girls, although it is high on my list of priorities.

我不會說『只是』為了把妹，不過那確實是我優先考慮的事情之一。
Kent

It’s pretty sad when you have to leave the country to find a girl that will date you.

當你得離開這個國家才能找到願意跟你約會的女生，這還真是有點可悲。
Jared

You’re so not funny. Maybe I should take you along and have you mummified. That way when an archaeologist digs you up in a thousand years, someone might actually be interested in what you have to say.

你一點都不好笑。也許我應該帶你一起去，把你做成木乃伊。這樣一千年後考古學家把你挖出來時，說不定才會有人真的對你說的話感興趣。

Unit 10: Woman’s Work

⬇ PDF
Annie

What does your father do?

你爸爸是做什麼的？
Jessica

He works in an office.

他在辦公室上班。
Annie

That is pretty general. Lots of people work in an office. Can you be more specific?

那說得太籠統了。很多人都在辦公室上班。你可以說得更具體一點嗎？
Jessica

He works in the accounting department at the Pretend Bicycle Company factory.

他在假腳踏車公司工廠的會計部門工作。
Annie

So he is an accountant?

所以他是一位會計師嗎？
Jessica

Not exactly. He takes phone calls, schedules meetings, and takes care of incoming mail.

不完全是。他負責接電話、安排會議，還有處理進來的郵件。
Annie

Oh! He is a secretary.

喔！他是一位秘書。
Jessica

He prefers to be called an office assistant. He gets mad when people call him a secretary. He says only women are called secretaries.

他比較喜歡被稱為辦公室助理。有人叫他秘書時他會生氣。他說只有女生才會被稱為秘書。
Annie

I see.

我懂了。
Jessica

And what does your dad do?

那你爸爸是做什麼的呢？
Annie

Don’t laugh, but he is a nurse. Jessica (laughing): Sorry. It just sounds funny to call a man a nurse. Annie (frowning angrily): I told you not to laugh. At least my dad isn’t a secretary!

別笑喔，但他是一位護士。潔西卡（笑著說）：抱歉。把一個男生叫做護士聽起來就是很好笑。安妮（生氣地皺著眉）：我叫你不要笑了。至少我爸爸不是秘書！
Jessica

Again, I’m sorry. It is funny, though, that both of our dads have “women’s” jobs.

再說一次，對不起。不過說真的，我們兩個的爸爸都從事「女性」的工作，還真有趣。
Annie

I wonder if their friends tease them about it.

我很好奇他們的朋友會不會因此取笑他們。
Jessica

Probably just behind their backs. The girls snicker at the thought of their dads being teased like school boys.

大概只會在背後偷偷取笑吧。女孩們一想到自己的爸爸像小男生一樣被取笑，就忍不住竊笑起來。
