Unit 1: Andy’s Computer Problems

⬇ PDF
Andy

Darn it! My computer froze again.

該死！我的電腦又當機了。
Tony

Why does it keep doing that?

為什麼它一直這樣？
Andy

Someone sent a file to my email that had a virus attached to it and I opened it.

有人寄了一個附加病毒的檔案到我的信箱，我把它打開了。
Tony

So your computer keeps freezing?

所以你的電腦一直當機？
Andy

Yeah, but that’s not all. Windows pop up repeatedly, programs close without notice, and files seem to be disappearing.

對啊，而且不只這樣。視窗會不停跳出來，程式無預警就關閉，檔案好像也一直在消失。
Tony

Sounds like your computer has a mind of its own.

聽起來你的電腦好像有自己的想法。
Andy

You can say that again. But what can I do?

一點沒錯。但我該怎麼辦？
Tony

I think you need to invest in some anti-virus software.

我覺得你需要買一套防毒軟體來投資一下。
Andy

Man, that is such a scam. You know it is the people who sell the software that make the viruses, don’t you?

天啊，那根本是個騙局。你知道賣軟體的人其實就是製造病毒的人吧？
Tony

Maybe, but what choice do you have?

或許吧，但你還有別的選擇嗎？
Andy

I’ll just reformat my hard drive and reinstall the operating system. That’ll fix it.

我乾脆重新格式化硬碟，再重新安裝作業系統，這樣就能解決了。
Tony

Wouldn’t it be easier just to buy the software?

買軟體不是比較簡單嗎？
Andy

Possibly, but this way is more reliable. After that I’ll just have to be more careful opening things that people send to me.

也許吧，但這樣做比較可靠。之後我打開別人寄給我的東西時也會更小心。
Tony

Suit yourself.

隨便你囉。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

What keeps happening to Andy’s computer?

It keeps freezing up.

How did he get a virus?

He opened an email that had a virus attached to it.

What does Tony think Andy should do?

Tony thinks Andy should buy anti-virus software.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

darn(int.)該死；真氣人Darn it, I forgot my umbrella again.該死，我又忘了帶傘。freeze(v.)不動My computer will freeze if I open too many programs at once.如果我一次開太多程式，我的電腦就會當機。virus(n.)病毒The doctor said it was just a common virus, not anything serious.醫生說這只是一般的病毒，沒有什麼大礙。attach(v.)附加Please attach your resume to the email before you send it.寄出郵件前請把履歷附加上去。notice(n.)通知；預告The store closed without any notice to its customers.這家店沒有給顧客任何通知就關門了。anti-virus(adj.)防毒的You should install anti-virus software to protect your computer.你應該安裝防毒軟體來保護你的電腦。software(n.)軟體This software makes it much easier to edit photos.這套軟體讓編輯照片變得容易許多。scam(n.)騙局That email asking for your bank password is definitely a scam.那封要求你提供銀行密碼的信絕對是個騙局。reformat(v.)重新格式化He had to reformat his laptop after it got infected with a virus.他的筆電中毒之後，只好重新格式化。hard drive(ph.)硬碟I backed up all my photos onto an external hard drive.我把所有照片都備份到一個外接硬碟裡了。reinstall(v.)重新安裝After the crash, she had to reinstall the operating system from scratch.當機之後，她只好從頭重新安裝作業系統。operating system(ph.)作業系統This computer is running an old operating system that is no longer supported.這台電腦執行的是一個已經不再支援的舊作業系統。reliable(adj.)可靠的This brand of laptop is known for being very reliable.這個牌子的筆電以非常可靠聞名。suit yourself(ph.)隨便你If you do not want to come with us, suit yourself.如果你不想跟我們一起去，隨便你。you can say that again(ph.)一點沒錯；你說得太對了This soup is delicious. You can say that again!這湯真好喝。一點沒錯！

完整教學音檔

📖 課文教學（中文講解）

小測驗 Quick Check

1. Why does Andy think anti-virus software is a scam?

2. What does Andy decide to do instead of just buying anti-virus software?

3. How does Andy plan to change his behavior after this experience?

Unit 2: The Handy Backscratcher

⬇ PDF
Eric

Can you give me a hand with something?

你可以幫我一個忙嗎？
Coco

Sure. (She begins clapping.)

當然。（她開始鼓掌。）
Eric

Oh thanks, you are hilarious.

喔，謝了，你真好笑。
Coco

Okay. Okay. What do you want help with?

好啦，好啦。你需要什麼幫忙？
Eric

I have a really itchy back and I can’t reach it to scratch. Can you scratch it for me?

我的背好癢，可是我搆不到抓不到。你可以幫我抓一下嗎？
Coco

Yeah, right! I wouldn’t touch you with a ten-foot pole. But I do happen to have a backscratcher in my backpack. (She hands him the backscratcher.) Here you go.

說得美！我才不會用十呎長竿碰你呢。不過我背包裡剛好有一支長柄扒。（她把長柄扒遞給他。）拿去吧。
Eric

(He grabs the backscratcher and starts scratching his back like a mad man.) Thanks. That feels much better.

（他一把抓過長柄扒，像瘋子一樣抓起背來。）謝了，這樣舒服多了。
Coco

You owe me one. Remember the old saying: You scratch my back and I’ll scratch yours.

你欠我一次。記住那句老話：你抓我的背，我就抓你的背。
Eric

Right. I won’t forget.

好啦，我不會忘的。
Coco

You’d better not forget, if you know what’s good for you. Questions: What is the backscratcher made from? Why is Eric looking for help? Does Coco want to scratch his itch for him? Why not?

你最好別忘了，為了你自己好。
課文朗讀（真人）

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

handy(adj.)方便的A backscratcher is a handy tool for reaching your back.長柄扒是一種方便觸及背部的工具。backscratcher(n.)長柄扒He used a backscratcher to scratch the itchy spot on his back.他用長柄扒抓背上發癢的地方。bamboo(n.)竹子The backscratcher is made of bamboo.這支長柄扒是用竹子做的。flat(adj.)平的One end of the backscratcher is flat.長柄扒的一端是平的。curved(adj.)彎曲的The other end is curved like a small hand.另一端則彎曲得像一隻小手。end(n.)末端She held the end of the backscratcher tightly.她緊緊握住長柄扒的末端。shape(v.)製成…的形狀The bamboo is shaped like a tiny hand.那根竹子被製成一隻小手的形狀。scratch(v.)抓(癢)Can you scratch my back for me?你可以幫我抓背嗎？hard-to-reach(adj.)難以觸及的The backscratcher is great for hard-to-reach places.長柄扒很適合用在難以觸及的地方。itch(n.)癢He has an itch right in the middle of his back.他背部正中間有個癢處。wonder(v.)想知道I wonder what this tool is used for.我想知道這個工具是用來做什麼的。a hand(ph.)幫忙；鼓掌Can you give me a hand with this box?你可以幫我搬這個箱子嗎？hilarious(adj.)極可笑的That joke you told was absolutely hilarious.你剛才說的那個笑話真的太好笑了。happen to(ph.)碰巧I happen to have an extra pen in my bag.我碰巧包包裡有一支多的筆。grab(v.)拿He reached over and grabbed the backscratcher.他伸手拿過長柄扒。owe(v.)欠(債或人情)You owe me a favor after this.這件事之後你欠我一個人情。had better(ph.)最好You had better finish your homework before dinner.你最好在晚餐前把功課做完。

完整教學音檔

📖 課文教學（中文講解）

小測驗 Quick Check

1. What does Coco use to make Eric think she is helping him at first?

2. Where does Coco keep the backscratcher?

3. What does Coco say she would not do to Eric?

Unit 3: Sharing

⬇ PDF
Grace

Umm! This chocolate bar is so good.

嗯！這條巧克力棒真好吃。
Julie

Can I have some?

可以給我一些嗎？
Grace

No way! You never share with me when you have a treat.

不可能！你有好東西的時候從來不跟我分享。
Julie

That’s because you’re a pig and always take too much.

那是因為你是個貪吃鬼，而且總是拿太多。
Grace

I am not a pig. You are!

我才不是貪吃鬼。你才是！
Julie

(Starts to cry.) You are so mean. I am your little sister and you should take care of 5me.

（開始哭了起來。）你好壞心。我是你的妹妹，你應該要照顧我。
Grace

Okay, okay. Stop your crying. Here you go. Grace hands her a piece of chocolate.

好啦，好啦。別哭了。給你。葛瑞絲遞給她一塊巧克力。
Julie

You are the best sister ever.

妳是有史以來最好的姊姊。
Grace

Remember that the next time I want some of your candy.

下次我想要吃妳的糖果時，記得這件事。
Julie

I will. Don’t worry.

我會的。別擔心。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

Why does Julie start to cry?

She starts to cry because her big sister is being mean to her.

Why does Julie say that Grace is the best sister ever?

Because Grace finally gives her a piece of the chocolate.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

sharing(n.)分享Sharing your toys with friends is a kind thing to do.把你的玩具分享給朋友是一件善良的事。chocolate bar(ph.)巧克力棒She bought a chocolate bar at the store after school.她放學後在商店買了一條巧克力棒。treat(n.)好吃的東西Mom gave us a treat for finishing our homework.媽媽給我們一個好吃的東西作為完成作業的獎勵。mean(adj.)吝嗇的；壞心的It is mean to laugh at someone who falls down.嘲笑跌倒的人是很壞心的。take care of(ph.)照顧The older brother has to take care of his little sister.哥哥必須照顧他的妹妹。hand(v.)交給Please hand me the book on the table.請把桌上的那本書交給我。no way(ph.)不可能；才不要No way, I am not giving you my last cookie.不可能，我才不要把我最後一塊餅乾給你。share(v.)分享；共用You should share your snacks with your classmates.你應該把你的零食分享給同學。pig(n.)貪吃鬼；豬Do not be such a pig and leave some cake for everyone else.不要那麼貪吃，留一些蛋糕給大家。too much(ph.)太多He always takes too much food and leaves little for others.他總是拿太多食物，留給別人的很少。cry(v.)哭The little girl started to cry when she lost her toy.那個小女孩弄丟玩具時開始哭了起來。little sister(ph.)妹妹My little sister is five years younger than me.我妹妹比我小五歲。here you go(ph.)給你；拿去吧Here you go, this is the pencil you were looking for.給你，這是你在找的那支鉛筆。remember(v.)記得Please remember to bring your umbrella tomorrow.明天請記得帶雨傘。candy(n.)糖果The children ate a lot of candy during the holiday.孩子們在假期吃了很多糖果。

完整教學音檔

📖 課文教學（中文講解）

小測驗 Quick Check

1. What kind of treat is Grace eating at the start of the dialogue?

2. What does Grace say her little sister always does with treats?

3. What does Grace ask her little sister to remember for next time?

Unit 4: Moon Festival BBQ

⬇ PDF
Joe

What did you do for the Moon Festival?

你中秋節做了什麼？
Ted

I went to my parent’s house for a BBQ.

我去我爸媽家烤肉。
Joe

How was that?

那怎麼樣？
Ted

It was great. My mother prepared a lot of different foods for us to eat.

很棒。我媽媽準備了很多不同的食物給我們吃。
Joe

Is your mother a good cook?

你媽媽很會做菜嗎？
Ted

Yes, she is, but she only prepared the food. My father actually did the cooking on the BBQ as he always does. He thinks he is the best.

是啊，她很會做菜，但她只負責準備食材。實際上都是我爸爸在烤肉架上做菜，他一直都是這樣。他覺得自己是最厲害的。
Joe

That’s funny! My dad is the same way. He thinks he is the only one that can cook on a BBQ.

太好笑了！我爸也是這樣。他覺得自己是唯一一個會用烤肉架做菜的人。
Ted

Yeah. I think everyone’s dad thinks that they are the best BBQ-er.

對啊。我覺得每個人的爸爸都覺得自己是最棒的烤肉高手。
Joe

True, but it is always the moms left to clean up the mess.

沒錯，但最後都是媽媽們被留下來收拾殘局。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

What did Ted do during the Moon Festival?

He went to his parent’s house for a BBQ.

Who did the cooking?

Ted’s father did the cooking.

What do both of their dads think?

They both think that they are the best at cooking on the BBQ.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

Moon Festival(ph.)中秋節Every year, our family gets together for the Moon Festival.每年中秋節，我們全家都會團聚在一起。BBQ(abbr.)=barbecue烤肉We are having a BBQ in the backyard this weekend.我們這個週末要在後院烤肉。actually(adv.)實際上I actually enjoy cooking on the weekend.我其實蠻喜歡在週末做菜的。clean up(ph.)清理Can you help me clean up the kitchen after dinner?晚餐後你可以幫我清理廚房嗎？mess(n.)髒亂The kids left a big mess in the living room.孩子們把客廳弄得一團亂。prepare(v.)準備My mother always prepares a lot of food for the holidays.我媽媽在節日總是準備很多食物。cook(n.)廚師；會做菜的人My grandmother is a wonderful cook.我奶奶是一位很棒的廚師。cook(v.)烹飪；做菜My father likes to cook on the BBQ every weekend.我爸爸每個週末都喜歡在烤肉架上做菜。the same way(ph.)有一樣的想法；同樣如此My brother thinks he is the best singer, and my cousin is the same way.我哥哥覺得他是最棒的歌手，我表哥也是一樣的想法。the best(ph.)最棒的（人或事物）He always says his cooking is the best in the family.他總是說他的廚藝是全家最棒的。left to(ph.)被留下來（做某事）After the party, she was left to clean up all the dishes.派對結束後，她被留下來洗所有的碗盤。funny(adj.)有趣的；好笑的That is a really funny story about your dad.你爸爸的那個故事真的很好笑。great(adj.)很棒的The food at the party was great.派對上的食物很棒。a lot of(ph.)很多的She cooked a lot of different dishes for the festival.她為了節慶做了很多不同的菜。the only one(ph.)唯一的一個（人）He thinks he is the only one who can fix the car.他覺得他是唯一一個會修車的人。

完整教學音檔

📖 課文教學（中文講解）

小測驗 Quick Check

1. Where did the speaker go for the Moon Festival?

2. What did the mother do for the BBQ?

3. According to the dialogue, who usually cleans up the mess after the BBQ?

Unit 5: Buying Bread

⬇ PDF
Jack

I love the smell of a bakery.

我喜歡麵包店的味道。
Irene

Me, too. Every time I come here, my mouth starts to water as soon as I walk through the door. So, what do you need to get?

我也是。每次我來這裡，一走進門我就開始流口水。那，你需要買什麼？
Jack

I need to buy some bread so that I can make a sandwich for my lunch tomorrow. Where is it?

我需要買一些麵包，這樣明天午餐我才能做三明治。麵包在哪裡？
Irene

I think the bread is over by the pastries. They find the area where the bread is.

我想麵包就在糕點區旁邊。他們找到了放麵包的區域。
Jack

Wow. There are so many different kinds of bread. It makes it hard to choose.

哇，有好多不同種類的麵包，讓人很難選擇。
Irene

What is this long one?

這條長長的是什麼？
Jack

That is garlic bread. It has butter and garlic already inside. You heat it up in the oven and then slice it. It is usually served with steak, pasta or Caesar salad.

那是大蒜麵包。裡面已經有奶油和大蒜了。你把它放進烤箱加熱，然後切片。它通常會配牛排、義大利麵或凱薩沙拉一起上桌。
Irene

I think I’ve had it before, but I don’t really like garlic that much. What are these ones that look like a bull’s horns?

我想我以前吃過，但我不太喜歡大蒜。那些看起來像牛角的是什麼？
Jack

They are called croissants. They are very popular for breakfast. They are usually heated and then you can put some butter on them. Sometimes, people even slice them in half and make sandwiches. Tuna salad and crab salad croissants are both delicious and great light lunches.

它們叫可頌。它們是很受歡迎的早餐選擇。通常會先加熱，然後你可以在上面塗一些奶油。有時候，人們甚至會把它們對半切開做成三明治。鮪魚沙拉和蟹肉沙拉可頌都很美味，也是很棒的清淡午餐。
Irene

Look here! You can even get hamburger and hot dog buns here. They are quite hard to find in Taiwan. Maybe I should pick up a dozen hot dog buns and we can have a wiener roast at the beach this weekend.

你看這裡！這裡甚至有賣漢堡和熱狗麵包。這些東西在台灣很難找到。也許我應該買一打熱狗麵包，這個週末我們可以在海邊辦一場烤香腸聚會。
Jack

That sounds like a great idea. Jack takes a couple loaves of bread off the shelf.

聽起來是個好主意。傑克從架上拿了幾條吐司。
Irene

Is that all you need?

這樣就是你需要的全部了嗎？
Jack

Yep. One loaf of white bread and one whole-wheat should get me through the week.

對啊。一條白吐司和一條全麥吐司應該就夠我吃過這一週了。
Irene

Alright. We can pay and go home.

好。我們可以結帳回家了。
Jack

On second thought, maybe I should grab a half a dozen doughnuts…as long as we are here.

再想一想，也許既然我們都來了，我應該拿半打甜甜圈……
Irene

I knew you couldn’t resist buying something sweet.

我就知道你無法抗拒買些甜的東西。
Jack

You know me too well.

你太了解我了。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

Why did Jack need to go to the bakery?

He needed to buy some bread to make sandwiches for his lunch the next day.

Why was Mimi surprised to see hot dog and hamburger buns there?

She was surprised because they are quite hard to find in Taiwan.

Was Mimi surprised that Jack wanted to buy some doughnuts?

Sure! My money is on the green tweed jacket and the paisley tie.

好啊！我賭他會穿綠色花呢外套配佩斯利渦旋紋領帶。
Joe

Ok. You’re on.

好。一言為定。
Dan

What do you want to wager?

你想賭什麼？
Joe

How about, if I win, you have to do my homework for a week?

這樣好了，如果我贏了，你要幫我做一個星期的功課？
Dan

And what if I win? I don’t need YOU to do MY homework. I don’t want to fail my class!

那如果我贏了呢？我才不需要你幫我做功課咧。我可不想被當掉！
Joe

Ok. How about if you win, I’ll buy you dinner at MOS Burger.

好吧。如果你贏了，我請你去摩斯漢堡吃晚餐。
Dan

Make it two dinners and you’ve got yourself a deal. The two shake hands just as Mr. Johnson walks into the classroom. Joe jumps into the air cheering.

那就請兩頓晚餐，這樣才算成交。就在強森老師走進教室的那一刻，兩人握手成交。喬興奮地跳到半空中歡呼。
Joe

YES! Red blazer, yellow tie! I win. You can pay up by finishing last night’s homework.

太好了！紅色西裝外套、黃色領帶！我贏了。你可以把昨晚的功課寫完來付清賭債。
Dan

Great.

好吧。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

What does Joe think Louie will wear to class?

He thinks Louie will wear a red blazer and yellow tie.

How do they make a game out of what their teacher will wear?

They make a bet.

What does Dan wager?

He wagers to do Joe’s homework if he loses.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

blazer(n.)西裝外套；運動夾克Joe wore a blue blazer to the school assembly.喬穿了一件藍色西裝外套去參加學校集會。in a row(ph.)連續地He wore the same shirt three days in a row.他連續三天穿同一件襯衫。tweed(n.)花呢套裝Mr. Johnson always wears a brown tweed jacket in winter.強森老師冬天總是穿一件棕色花呢外套。paisley(adj.)佩斯利渦旋紋花呢的She gave her father a paisley tie for his birthday.她送給爸爸一條佩斯利渦旋紋的領帶當生日禮物。wager(v.)打賭；下賭注I will wager five dollars that our team wins the game.我要下注五元賭我們這隊會贏得比賽。deal(n.)交易If you help me clean the room, we have a deal.如果你幫我打掃房間，那我們就成交了。bet(n.)賭注；打賭Dan made a bet with his friend about the clothes their teacher would wear.丹跟朋友就老師會穿什麼衣服打了一個賭。walk into(ph.v.)走進The students stood up when the principal began to walk into the room.校長走進房間時，學生們都站了起來。pay up(ph.v.)付清（欠款）；乖乖付錢You lost the bet, so it is time to pay up.你賭輸了，該乖乖付錢了。cheer(v.)歡呼；喝采The whole class began to cheer when their team won.當他們那隊獲勝時，全班同學都歡呼了起來。shake hands(ph.)握手The two boys agreed to shake hands on the bet.兩個男孩握手同意了這個賭約。fail(v.)不及格；失敗She studied hard because she did not want to fail the test.她努力讀書，因為她不想考試不及格。jump(v.)跳；跳躍The children began to jump up and down with excitement.孩子們興奮得又跳又叫。tie(n.)領帶He chose a yellow tie to match his blazer.他選了一條黃色領帶來搭配他的西裝外套。probably(adv.)大概；可能It will probably rain later this afternoon.今天下午稍晚可能會下雨。

Unit 10: The Crush

⬇ PDF
Leo

So, I heard you have a crush on Frieda.

所以,我聽說你迷戀弗麗達。
Benson

Really? Where did you hear that?

真的嗎?你從哪裡聽來的?
Leo

Angel told me all about it at school yesterday.

安琪兒昨天在學校把整件事都告訴我了。
Benson

How would she know?

她怎麼會知道?
Leo

Because Alice overheard you telling Tony that she was hot.

因為愛麗絲無意中聽到你跟東尼說她很正。
Benson

Well Alice doesn’t know what she is talking about. What other rumors is she saying about me?

呃,愛麗絲根本不知道自己在說什麼。她還說了我什麼其他的謠言?
Leo

She also said that you want to ask Frieda to the dance.

她還說你想邀弗麗達去參加舞會。
Benson

WHAT!? I didn’t say anything about asking her to the dance. She gossips too much. Doesn’t she have anything better to do than spread lies about me?

什麼!?我根本沒說過要邀她去舞會的事。她太愛講八卦了。她除了到處散播關於我的謊言之外,難道沒有更好的事情可做嗎?
Leo

I guess not. You know what girls are like.

我猜沒有吧。你知道女生就是這樣。
Benson

She should just mind her own business.

她應該管好自己的事就好。
Leo

Maybe, but is there any truth to the rumors. Benson (stuttering): Uh… Of course there isn’t.

也許吧,但這些謠言有沒有一點是真的?班森(結巴地):呃……當然沒有。
Leo

The stutter in your voice seems to say otherwise.

你說話結巴的樣子,聽起來可不像是這麼一回事。
Benson

All I said was that she was cute. That’s it!

我只是說她很可愛,就這樣而已!
Leo

You didn’t say anything about asking her to the dance?

你完全沒說過要邀她去舞會的事嗎?
Benson

Umm. Uh!

嗯。呃!
Leo

So it is true then. You do have a crush on her. Benson (blushing): Alright, I give up. I’m secretly in love with Frieda.

所以這是真的了。你真的迷戀她。班森(臉紅地):好啦,我認輸。我偷偷愛上弗麗達了。
Leo

Why didn’t you just say so in the first place?

你為什麼一開始不直接說呢?
Benson

Because I don’t want anybody to know.

因為我不想讓任何人知道。
Leo

Well, the whole school already knows. Even Frieda knows.

呃,全校都已經知道了。連弗麗達自己都知道。
Benson

I feel so embarrassed. I am going to die now. Just put me out of my misery.

我覺得好尷尬。我要羞死了。乾脆讓我死了算了。
Leo

If it makes you feel any better, I think I have a crush on Joy.

如果這樣能讓你好過一點,老實說我覺得我也迷戀喬伊。
Benson

It does make me feel better knowing that I am not the only one with a crush.

知道我不是唯一一個迷戀別人的人,確實讓我好過多了。
Leo

We all go through it at some time. My dad says that it is a part of growing up.

我們每個人都會經歷這個階段。我爸說這是成長必經的過程。
Benson

Thanks for sharing that.

謝謝你告訴我這些。
Leo

No problem. Anything to help a friend.

不客氣。為朋友做什麼都願意。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

What rumors are going around school about Benson?

The rumors are that he has a crush on Frieda and that he is going to ask her out.

Why does Benson try to hide his feelings about Frieda?

He tries to hide his feelings because he doesn’t want anybody to know.

What makes Benson feel better?

When Leo tells him that he has a crush on Joy.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

crush(n.)迷戀I have a crush on a boy in my class.我迷戀班上的一個男生。overhear(v.)無意中聽到I did not mean to overhear your conversation.我不是故意無意中聽到你們的對話。rumor(n.)謠言；傳聞There is a rumor that the two of them are dating.有謠言說他們兩個在交往。gossip(v.)說長道短；聊八卦They like to gossip about their classmates during lunch.他們喜歡在午餐時間聊同學的八卦。spread(v.)傳播Do not spread rumors that are not true.不要傳播不實的謠言。stutter(v.)結巴地說He began to stutter when the teacher asked him a question.老師問他問題時,他開始結巴地說話。otherwise(adv.)不同地He said he was fine, but his face seemed to say otherwise.他說他沒事,但他的表情似乎透露出不同的訊息。blush(v.)臉紅She started to blush when he complimented her.他稱讚她時,她開始臉紅。secretly(adv.)秘密地He secretly hoped that she would say yes.他暗自希望她會答應。in the first place(ph.)一開始Why did you not tell me the truth in the first place?你為什麼一開始不告訴我實話呢?misery(n.)不幸；悲慘Losing the game brought the whole team a lot of misery.輸掉比賽為整個團隊帶來了很多痛苦。go through(ph.)經歷Every teenager has to go through this awkward stage.每個青少年都必須經歷這個尷尬的階段。hot(adj.)性感的；很有魅力的（俚語）All the students think the new transfer student is hot.所有學生都覺得那位新轉學生很有魅力。embarrassed(adj.)尷尬的；不好意思的She felt embarrassed when everyone stared at her.當大家都盯著她看時,她感到很尷尬。mind one's own business(ph.)少管閒事；管好自己的事You should learn to mind your own business instead of asking about other people all the time.你應該學會少管閒事,別老是打聽別人的事。
