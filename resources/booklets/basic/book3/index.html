Unit 1: How to Make a Scarecrow

⬇ PDF

This is a scarecrow. It is standing alone in a field. Scarecrows are used to scare away birds that may eat crops. They are supposed to look like people standing in the field. If the birds think the field is inhabited, they will probably not stop there.

To make a scarecrow all you need are a couple of poles, some straw and an old shirt. First, you make a cross out of the poles and tie them together. Then you put a shirt on the cross and stuff it with straw. Next you need something that looks like a head to put on the top. Finally, put a hat on the head and you’re finished. Now all you have to do is to stick it in the ground in your field or garden.

Without scarecrows, some farmers may resort to shooting or poisoning birds. In reality, scarecrows save crops and birds. Everyone is a winner.

課文朗讀（真人）
這是一個稻草人，它獨自地站在稻田裡。稻草人被用來嚇走可能會吃農作物的鳥類。它們看起來應該要像是有人站在田裡的樣子。如果鳥類以為田裡有人居住，它們可能就不會在那裡停留。 做一個稻草人只需要兩根竿子、一些乾草和一件舊襯衫。首先，用竿子做成一個十字架，並且把它們綁起來。然後把襯衫穿在十字架上面，並在裡面塞進乾草。接下來，你需要將一個看起來像頭部的東西放在頂端。最後，放一頂帽子在頭上就大功告成了。現在，你只需將它插在你田裡或菜園的土裡即可。 不用稻草人的話，有些農夫可能會用射殺或毒害小鳥的方式。事實上，稻草人拯救了農作物和鳥類。每個人都是贏家。

閱讀理解 Questions

Why do farmers put scarecrows up?

They put them up to scare away birds.

What does a scarecrow look like to a bird?

It looks like a person standing in the field.

What things do you need to make a scarecrow?

You need a couple of poles, some straw, an old shirt, a hat and something that looks like a head.

Unit 2: The Claw Game

⬇ PDF

Luke is trying to win a stuffed toy for his daughter. To operate the claw vending machine, 3 he first needs to insert a coin. When he hears the music start, it's time for action. He uses the joystick to maneuver the claw forward, backward, left and right. When the claw is positioned over top of the toy, he hits the button on the right. The open claw then drops down and attempts to grab a prize. At this point the claw, and Luke’s prize, begin to return to the top of the machine. Usually when the grapple hook gets to the top, it drops the item and ends your turn, but not this time. The claw has a firm hold on the stuffed toy, it then moves toward the hole on the left side. Luke is excited because he has never had a toy make it this far. The claw slowly opens and drops the prize into the hole. Luke reaches in and proudly claims his prize. More often than not, people walk away without a toy and without any change left in their pockets, but on this day, Luke was one of the lucky ones.

Unit 3: Correcting Your Mistakes

⬇ PDF

When making study notes, it is a good idea to keep them neat and legible. Sloppy notes will only lead to confusion and lost time when you study. Try to use pencil as much as possible because it is easier to correct mistakes and then when you do mess up, you can simply erase the mistake and write it again. This will help to keep your work nice and neat.

If you must work with a pen, or are working with photocopied materials, using an eraser is not an option. In these cases you must use either correction fluid or correction tape for your mistakes. I find correction tape the tidiest and you can write over it almost as soon as it is applied, too. Correction fluid is okay as well, but it will require drying time and doesn’t look quite as nice. Sometimes dried bits of liquid paper end up on your work, making it difficult to write neatly. But in a pinch, I suppose, it is better than nothing.

課文朗讀（真人）
當你在做筆記時，保持字跡工整與易讀是很好的。當你在讀書時，筆記的字跡潦草只會導致困惑，而且浪費時間。儘量使用鉛筆，因為錯誤比較容易修正，而且當你的筆記弄亂時，你可以簡單地擦掉錯誤並且重寫。這將有助於使你的筆記非常整齊。 假如你必須用原子筆，或是處理影印的資料，你是不能選擇使用橡皮擦的。在這種情形下，你必須使用修正液或修正帶來處理你的錯誤。我覺得使用修正帶是最整潔的方式，而且你還可以在用了修正帶後，馬上寫上去。修正液也可以使用，但是它需要變乾的時間，而且看起來不像用修正帶那樣地好看。有時候修正液乾掉後形成的小紙塊讓你更難寫得整齊。但我想在緊急情況時，這總比什麼都沒有要來得好。

閱讀理解 Questions

How should your study notes look?

They should look neat and legible.

What should you do with your mistakes?

You should correct/erase/change them.

Which is tidier, correction tape or correction fluid?

Correction tape is tidier.

Unit 4: A Pocketful of Change

⬇ PDF

Paul’s pocket was heavy with change after a day of shopping. He reached his hand into his pocket and grabbed handful after handful of change, placing it on the coffee table. Sitting on the comfy armchair, he began to stack the change. He put the one dollar coins in stacks of 10 to make them easier to count. After he sorted them all out, he added them up. He had nearly 200 dollars in change. It was no wonder his pocket felt so heavy. He took the change and put it in his piggybank to save it for a day when he would want to buy something.

課文朗讀（真人）
購物了一整天之後，保羅的口袋因為裝零錢變得很重。他把手伸進他的口袋，然後拿出一把一把的零錢放在咖啡桌上。他坐在舒適的扶手椅上，開始把硬幣疊起來。他把一元的硬幣十個十個疊成堆，這樣比較容易數。在他把全部的硬幣分類好之後，他計算了零錢的總數。他的零錢將近有兩百元，難怪他的口袋感覺這麼重。他把零錢放入撲滿存起來，以便改天想買東西時就可以拿來用。

閱讀理解 Questions

Unit 5: The Dish Dryer

⬇ PDF

Lester has just finished washing the dishes. After he washes the dishes, he places each of them into the dish-dryer above the sink. The wire rack in the dryer holds plates and bowls on their side. This allows air to flow freely between the dishes as they dry. It also helps to organize the dishes so that you can fit more of them into the dryer. There is a spot on the side for utensils, too. It holds them upright, which allows water to drip off of them.

When the machine is fully loaded, he can turn it on, but he must close the door first. The glass door slides out from above and then swings down to cover the front of the unit. To turn it on, he needs to select the proper cycle and push the button. The machine will then dry the dishes with hot air.

The whole process takes about ten minutes or so. For the sake of energy conservation, you should try to make sure the machine is full before you turn it on.

課文朗讀（真人）
列斯特剛把碗盤洗好。他洗完之後，把碗盤一一的放到洗滌槽上面的烘碗機裡。烘碗機裡的金屬架可以讓碗盤側放，這樣一來，在烘乾的時候，空氣可以不受阻礙地在碗盤之間流動。這種方式也能將碗盤排放整齊，讓你能放更多的碗盤到烘碗機裡。在烘碗機的邊緣還有個放餐具的位置，餐具放得直直的，好讓水可以滴下來。 當烘碗機裝滿碗盤之後，他就可以打開電源了。但是在啟動之前，他得先把門關上才行。玻璃門從上方滑出，然後往下擺動蓋住烘碗機的前面。要啟動時，他必須選擇適合的循環模式，並且按下按鈕，機器便會開始用熱空氣來烘乾碗盤了。整個過程大概會花十分鐘左右。為了節約能源起見，在啟動之前，你應該先確認烘碗機是否有擺滿碗盤。

閱讀理解 Questions

How does Lester place the dishes in the dish rack?

He places them in on their side/edge.

Why are the dishes placed on their side?

They are placed on their side to allow the air to flow freely through them.

How long does the whole process take?

It takes about 10 minutes or so.

Unit 6: Andre Makes a Scrapbook

⬇ PDF

Hi, my name is Andre and I love collecting basketball memorabilia. I collect pins, hats, stickers, shirts, banners and anything I can get my hands on. In my spare time I have also made a scrapbook.

My favorite team has always been the Denver Nuggets, so the bulk of the book is of them. Every day I look through the sports sections of the local and national papers. If I find an interesting article about a Nuggets’ player, I cut it outand put it in my scrapbook. There are pages and pages of such newspaper clippings in my book.

As long as I can remember, Allen Iverson has been my favorite player. I actually have another scrapbook just for him. Inside this book there are pictures that I have collected from various magazines and papers. They span his entire career. I have pictures of his first pro game, his first all-star game, and even some of his personal life. As well, every milestone he has passed is documented in my book. I think I must be his biggest fan.

When I am old, I will be able to look back through my scrapbooks and remember my heroes in all their glory. Hopefully, my children will want to look at them, too. Maybe it will inspire them to start a scrapbook of their own.

Unit 7: Posing for a Picture

⬇ PDF

At one of the local markets, an artist has set up a shop. There are examples of his work hung on the walls for you to admire. If you want, he will draw your picture, too.

Posing for a picture will require that you sit very still. If you move around too much, it will be difficult for the artist to sketch your picture. As you sit, he will study the lines and shape of your face, and then capture them on paper. He will try to make you as beautiful as possible by enhancing your good features and maybe overlooking some of the bad ones. Don’t expect miracles, though, as there is only so much he can hide.

The whole process will take about 20 minutes or so. When he has finished, you will have immortalized yourself in a piece of art and can hang it on a wall in your house for all to see. Don’t buy too many pictures of yourself, though, as your family might get sick of looking at you on every wall in the house.

課文朗讀（真人）
在當地的一個市場裡，有一位藝術家設立一間工作室。牆上掛著許多他作品的樣本供你欣賞。如果你願意的話，他也可以為你畫一張畫像。 擺姿勢畫像時，你必須端坐不動。如果你動得太厲害，畫家很難為你畫一張素描。當你坐著時，他會仔細地觀察你臉上的線條及輪廓，然後將這些特點描繪在紙上。畫家會試著加強你的優點，也許也會忽略一些缺點，盡可能讓你看起來美麗動人。然而，別期望奇蹟的出現，因為他能掩飾的就只有這麼多。 整個素描的過程需要花費約二十分鐘左右。當他完成時，你將得以永恆不朽地活在藝術品中，而且可以把畫像掛在家中的牆上供大家欣賞。然而，千萬不要買太多自己的畫像，若是每一面牆都會看到你的畫像，你的家人也許會感到很厭煩。

Unit 8: Recycling Containers

⬇ PDF

Three red bins and a yellow basket are sitting along the wall in the back of a classroom. They are filled with recyclable materials, except for one which is used for garbage. They are part of the school’s recycling program.

Each bin and the basket holds something different. One is used for juice boxes and tetra packs. One is used for plastics, such as tea glasses and plastic bottles. The third holds regular garbage and the yellow basket is used for paper and cardboard.

Recycling waste is a good way to conserve valuable resources. Instead of cutting down more trees to make new paper, we can recycle waste paper and use it again. All sorts of things can be made from recycled materials. It also helps to keep landfill sites from overflowing by reducing the amount of garbage stored in them.

Here students are sorting the bins. Any garbage is removed and items are organized and bagged according to what they are and what can be recycled with them. They will then be taken to a recycling depot.

課文朗讀（真人）
教室後面的牆邊擺著三個紅色的桶子和一個黃色的籃子。他們裡面裝滿了回收的物品，除了一個是被用來裝垃圾的之外。這些是學校資源回收計畫的一部分。 每個桶子和籃子分別用來放不同的物品。一個用來裝果汁盒和利樂包；一個裝塑膠製品，像是茶杯和塑膠瓶；第三個桶子裝一般垃圾，而黃色的籃子則是用來放紙張和紙板的。 垃圾回收是保存珍貴資源的好方法。我們可以回收廢紙再度使用，而不是砍伐更多的樹木來製造新的紙張。回收的材料能做成各式各樣的東西，而且資源回收可以減少囤積在掩埋場的垃圾量，避免掩埋場的垃圾多到滿出來。 這裡有一群學生正把桶子內的東西加以分類。他們去除垃圾，再根據東西的種類以及可以回收的部分來處理裝袋。然後這些東西就被送到資源回收的地點。

Unit 9: Back It Up!

⬇ PDF

The computer prompt tells Alice to please insert a disc into the CD-ROM drive. She pushes the eject button and the tray slides out automatically. She puts the CD on the tray and gently pushes it in. The tray then slides back in and she can hear the disc begin to whirl as the computer reads it.

Alice has also saved some of her homework as a Word file on her memory stick. It is like a small portable hard drive. All you have to do to use it is to plug it into an unused USB port. Once connected, there will be an icon in “my computer” or on your taskbar that will open it in a separate window. You can then use the file as you normally would on your own computer.

Portable memory devices are a very convenient way of carrying your files around without having to burn them to CD, but they are not one-hundred percent reliable. With this in mind, you should always back up important documents or files somewhere else.

課文朗讀（真人）
電腦出現一個提示，要求愛莉絲將光碟放到光碟機。她按下退出鍵，托盤就自動滑出來了。她把光碟片放到托盤上面，並輕輕的將它推入。當托盤滑回去後，她可以聽到電腦讀取光碟片時，片子在旋轉的聲音。 愛莉絲也將一些家庭作業以Word檔存在她的隨身碟裡。隨身碟就像一種小型的可攜式硬碟。如果你要使用它，只需要將它插入未使用的USB孔中。一旦連結了，會有一個圖像出現在「我的電腦」或者在工作列上，然後有另一個視窗開啟隨身碟的內容。如此一來，你就可以使用隨身碟的檔案，就如同你平常在使用電腦上的檔案一樣。 可攜式的記憶裝置可讓你方便地攜帶檔案，而不用燒錄到光碟片。但是它們並非百分之百可靠。考慮到這個因素，你應該經常把重要的文件或檔案備份到其它的地方。

Unit 10: Water Dispensers

⬇ PDF

This is a coin-operated water dispenser. Because the water in Taiwan is not safe to use for drinking or cooking, many people rely on these machines for their potable water. They are a cheap alternative to buying bottled water.

These types of water dispensers are very simple to use. You only need to bring a suitable container and some change. Then you insert a coin into the slot on the machine, put the nozzle into your container and open the valve. Be sure your container is safe for potable water and that you use it only for this purpose.

When you are finished filling your container, turn off the valve and place the nozzle back into the holder. Hopefully you haven’t bought more water than you can carry home or you might have to drink it until it is light enough to carry.

課文朗讀（真人）
這是一個投幣式的供水器。因為台灣的水用來喝或烹調並不安全，所以很多人依賴這些機器來取得他們的飲用水。和購買瓶裝水相較之下，它們是便宜的替代品。 這類型的供水器很容易操作。你只需要帶一個適當的容器和一些零錢，然後將硬幣投入機器的投幣孔裡，接下來將噴嘴放入你的容器中，再打開活門即可。請確認你用來裝飲用水的容器是合乎安全的，而且絕對只能用在裝飲用水的這個用途。 當水裝滿容器時，關閉活門，並將噴嘴放回吊掛處。但願你買的水不會超過你能夠提回去的量，否則你可能必須喝掉一些，直到桶子輕到可以讓你提回去為止。

閱讀理解 Questions

Why do people in Taiwan use water dispensers?

They use water dispensers because the water here is not safe for drinking or cooking.

What are some other alternatives to using a water machine?

You can buy bottled water, use spring water, or boil tap water.

Where does the water in the machine come from?

It comes from the mountains on a water truck.
