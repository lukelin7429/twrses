Unit 1: Running Laps

⬇ PDF

Our junior high school really believes in promoting physical exercise for the benefit of the students. Every morning after assembly, students are required to run three laps around the track. Not only does this boost their metabolism throughout the day, but it also helps them to wake up and be more alert.

我們的國中非常重視促進學生的體育鍛鍊，對他們大有好處。每天朝會結束後，學生都被要求繞著跑道跑三圈。這不僅能提升他們一整天的新陳代謝，還有助於喚醒身體、讓精神更加靈活機靈。

Keeping the students alert is a major battle that teachers have to face every day. It is hard to keep students motivated for learning, especially in the morning when everyone has just crawled out of bed. Most teachers believe that running laps every day has a definite effect on students’ alertness, focus and also helps to improve their grades greatly. Additionally, doing laps also improves the overall health of students.

讓學生保持清醒機靈，是老師每天都要面對的一場硬仗。要讓學生對學習保持動力並不容易，尤其是在大家剛從床上爬起來的早晨。大多數老師都認為，每天跑步對學生的機靈度、專注力有明確的效果，也大大有助於提升他們的成績。此外，跑步也能改善學生的整體健康。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

How many laps are they required to run?

They are required to run 3 laps around the track.

What are the benefits of exercising before classes start?

It improves focus, alertness, and helps students to wake up. It also boosts metabolism.

What effect does exercise seem to have on grades?

It seems to improve grades greatly.

Unit 2: Peter and the Drinking Fountain

⬇ PDF

Peter has taken a break from class. His classroom is always very hot during the summer months and he needs to drink lots of water so that he doesn’t get dehydrated.  He has also been diligently studying all morning in the hot classroom and has worked up a thirst.

彼得暫時離開了教室休息一下。他的教室在夏天總是非常悶熱，所以他需要喝很多水，以免脫水。他整個早上都在悶熱的教室裡用功讀書，因此感到口渴難耐。

The thirsty boy wanders down the long hallway to the nearest water fountain. As he turns the knob on the top of the stainless steel fountain, water begins to stream out. He lets it run for a few seconds to cool it off. He then bends over and takes a sip. Ah, it refreshes him instantly and he continues slurping away.

這個口渴的男孩沿著長長的走廊漫步走向最近的飲水機。當他轉動不鏽鋼飲水機頂部的旋鈕時，水開始流了出來。他讓水流了幾秒鐘讓它變涼。接著他彎下腰，啜飲了一口。啊，這讓他立刻精神一振，於是他繼續咕嚕咕嚕地喝著。

The cool water quickly quenches his thirst and he wipes his mouth on his sleeve. Satisfied now, he slowly returns to class, trying to waste as much time as possible.

冰涼的水很快解了他的渴，他用袖子擦了擦嘴。現在心滿意足的他，慢慢地走回教室，儘量拖延時間。
課文朗讀（真人）

Unit 3: Bilingual Signs

⬇ PDF

Taiwanese schools are trying to become more and more bilingual all the time. A good way to do this is to make all existing signs English and Chinese like exit, bathroom and other common signs. Another way is to put signs in high traffic areas to increase exposure to English.

台灣的學校正努力變得越來越雙語化。一個好方法是把所有現有的標誌都改成英文和中文並列，像是出口、廁所和其他常見的標誌。另一個方法是在人潮眾多的區域張貼標語，以增加學生接觸英文的機會。

This particular school has done this by placing old adages on the stairs. It works quite well as students cannot go up the stairs without having to look at the sayings. They may choose not to read them for a while, but chances are after going up and down the steps repeatedly they will eventually read them all out of curiosity.

這所特定的學校就是這麼做的，他們把古老的格言貼在樓梯上。這個做法效果相當好，因為學生上下樓梯時不得不看到這些標語。他們一開始或許會選擇不去閱讀，但在反覆上下樓梯之後，很有可能出於好奇心最終會把它們全部讀完。

The signs have English on the left and Chinese on the right. They are also brightly colored to catch your eye.

這些標語左邊是英文，右邊是中文，而且顏色鮮明，非常吸引目光。
課文朗讀（真人）

Unit 5: No Cafeteria is No Problem

⬇ PDF

Most schools in Taiwan do not have cafeterias. Instead, they hire caterers to do all the cooking and bring it to the schools. The contracted caterers do not prepare the food on-site; they do it in their own kitchens. Once the food is ready, it is packed into containers and dropped off 7at the schools. Each container holds the same thing so that there is no worry of a mix-up as to which one goes where. Representatives from each class then pick up the food at the drop-off point and bring it back to their classrooms.

台灣大部分的學校都沒有自助餐廳。相反地，他們聘請供餐業者負責烹煮，再把食物送到學校。簽約的供餐業者不會在學校現場準備食物，而是在自己的廚房裡完成烹調。食物一旦準備好，就會被裝進容器裡，送到學校去。每個容器裝的東西都一樣，這樣就不用擔心搞混哪一份是哪一班的。接著，各班代表會到卸貨地點領取食物，再帶回自己的教室。

At our school, the lunch containers are put on chairs or tables at the front of the classroom near the chalkboard. Volunteers then distribute the food evenly amongst the students. If there are any leftovers, students are allowed to have second helpings. As you can see in the second picture, the students here are very hungry and can’t wait to dish up.

Unit 10: Bottled Water

⬇ PDF

When you are on the go, the best option to re-hydrate yourself is bottled water. It is readily available at most stores and there are countless brands and types of water to choose from. The number of choices is almost mind-boggling. You have distilled water, spring water, carbonated water and flavored water—just to name a few. Whatever their name, they all serve the same purpose: quenching your thirst.

當你外出忙碌時，補充水分最好的選擇就是瓶裝水。幾乎每家商店都能輕易買到瓶裝水，而且品牌與種類多得數不清。可選擇的數量之多，簡直令人驚訝。你可以選擇蒸餾水、泉水、碳酸水，還有各種口味的水——這裡僅列出幾種。無論名稱為何，它們的用途都相同：解渴。

The lady pictured here has just unscrewed the lid on her bottle of water. When buying bottled water, make sure the safety seal is not broken, so that you can be sure that the water hasn’t been tampered with and is fresh. If the seal is broken, it is best not to drink it just to be on the safe side.

圖中這位女士剛轉開她那瓶水的瓶蓋。購買瓶裝水時，務必確認安全封口沒有被破壞，這樣你才能確定這瓶水沒有被動過手腳，而且是新鮮的。如果封口已經破損，為了安全起見，最好不要喝。

In the second picture, our beautiful young model is taking a drink. She tips back the bottle and starts guzzling it down like there’s no tomorrow. She seems to be very thirsty. Perhaps she was lost in the desert and was unable to find water to quench her thirst. After finishing, she tosses the empty bottle into the recycling bin.

在第二張照片中，我們美麗的年輕模特兒正在喝水。她把瓶子往後傾斜，開始像沒有明天似地牛飲起來。她看起來非常口渴。也許她曾經迷失在沙漠中，找不到水來解渴。喝完之後，她把空瓶丟進資源回收桶。
課文朗讀（真人）
