Unit 1: Running Laps

⬇ PDF

Our junior high school really believes in promoting physical exercise for the benefit of the students. Every morning after assembly, students are required to run three laps around the track. Not only does this boost their metabolism throughout the day, but it also helps them to wake up and be more alert.

Keeping the students alert is a major battle that teachers have to face every day. It is hard to keep students motivated for learning, especially in the morning when everyone has just crawled out of bed. Most teachers believe that running laps every day has a definite effect on students’ alertness, focus and also helps to improve their grades greatly. Additionally, doing laps also improves the overall health of students.

課文朗讀（真人）
我們的國中十分相信提倡體能運動對學生有益。在每天早上的朝會過後，學生必須跑操場三圈。這不僅會促進他們一天的新陳代謝，也能幫助他們精神振作，並且變得更加靈敏。 讓學生保持靈敏是老師每天必須面對的重大挑戰。特別是早上學生剛從床上爬起來，要讓他們維持學習的動機是很困難的。大多數的老師相信每天跑操場對學生的靈活度與專注力有顯著的效果，並且能夠大大地提昇他們的成績。此外，跑操場也能改善學生整體的健康。

閱讀理解 Questions

How many laps are they required to run?

They are required to run 3 laps around the track.

What are the benefits of exercising before classes start?

It improves focus, alertness, and helps students to wake up. It also boosts metabolism.

What effect does exercise seem to have on grades?

It seems to improve grades greatly.

Unit 2: Peter and the Drinking Fountain

⬇ PDF

Peter has taken a break from class. His classroom is always very hot during the summer months and he needs to drink lots of water so that he doesn’t get dehydrated.  He has also been diligently studying all morning in the hot classroom and has worked up a thirst.

The thirsty boy wanders down the long hallway to the nearest water fountain. As he turns the knob on the top of the stainless steel fountain, water begins to stream out. He lets it run for a few seconds to cool it off. He then bends over and takes a sip. Ah, it refreshes him instantly and he continues slurping away.

The cool water quickly quenches his thirst and he wipes his mouth on his sleeve. Satisfied now, he slowly returns to class, trying to waste as much time as possible.

課文朗讀（真人）
彼得下課了。他的教室在夏天的這幾個月份都很熱，所以他需要補充很多水分才不至於脫水。他整個早上都在很熱的教室裡用功讀書，使得他很口渴。這個口渴的小男孩緩慢的沿著長廊走到了最近的飲水機。當他轉動不鏽鋼飲水機上面的轉鈕時，水就慢慢的流出來。他讓它流了幾秒，讓水涼一點才喝。然後他彎下腰去喝一小口。啊！這一小口使他立刻恢復精神，接著他繼續咕嚕咕嚕地喝。 那涼爽的水很快地解除他的口渴，然後他用袖子擦擦他的嘴巴。此刻，彼得感到很滿足。他緩慢的走回教室，試著要儘量拖延一些時間。

閱讀理解 Questions

What had Peter been doing that made him thirsty?

He had been diligently studying all morning.

He lets the water run to cool it off.

What does Peter wipe his mouth on?

He wipes his mouth on his sleeve.

Unit 3: Bilingual Signs

⬇ PDF

Taiwanese schools are trying to become more and more bilingual all the time. A good way to do this is to make all existing signs English and Chinese like exit, bathroom and other common signs. Another way is to put signs in high traffic areas to increase exposure to English.

This particular school has done this by placing old adages on the stairs. It works quite well as students cannot go up the stairs without having to look at the sayings. They may choose not to read them for a while, but chances are after going up and down the steps repeatedly they will eventually read them all out of curiosity.

The signs have English on the left and Chinese on the right. They are also brightly colored to catch your eye.

課文朗讀（真人）
台灣的學校一直試著要變得更為雙語化，而將目前所有的標誌做成中英對照是個好方法，例如：出口、洗手間，以及其它常見的標誌。另一個方式是把標誌放在人來人往的地方，以增加學生接觸英文的機會。 這所特定的學校把一些古老的諺語放在階梯來達成雙語化的目的。由於學生上樓梯時一定會看到這些諺語，所以它的效果很好。也許會有一段時間他們選擇不去看這些諺語，但是很可能因為要不斷的上下樓梯，最後只因出於好奇心而去閱讀它們。 這些標誌左邊是英文，右邊是中文。它們的色彩也很鮮明，以便能吸引你的目光。

閱讀理解 Questions

What is a good way to make schools bilingual?

A good way to do this is by making all existing signs English and Chinese.

Where should schools put the signs to increase exposure?

They should put them in high traffic areas to increase exposure.

Why are the old adages brightly colored?

They are brightly colored to catch your eye.

Unit 5: No Cafeteria is No Problem

⬇ PDF

Most schools in Taiwan do not have cafeterias. Instead, they hire caterers to do all the cooking and bring it to the schools. The contracted caterers do not prepare the food on-site; they do it in their own kitchens. Once the food is ready, it is packed into containers and dropped off 7at the schools. Each container holds the same thing so that there is no worry of a mix-up as to which one goes where. Representatives from each class then pick up the food at the drop-off point and bring it back to their classrooms.

At our school, the lunch containers are put on chairs or tables at the front of the classroom near the chalkboard. Volunteers then distribute the food evenly amongst the students. If there are any leftovers, students are allowed to have second helpings. As you can see in the second picture, the students here are very hungry and can’t wait to dish up.

Every student carries a small lunch bag that contains utensils and a stainless steel bowl. They pack their lunch bag with them every day, but if they happen to forget, there are disposable bowls and utensils available so that no one has to go hungry. After lunch, each student is responsible for cleaning his or her own dish.

課文朗讀（真人）
在台灣，大部分的學校都沒有自助餐廳，而是僱用辦理外食的廠商來幫他們料理飯菜，然後再送到學校來。和學校簽約的廠商並非在現場料理食材，而是在他們自己的廚房做。一旦食物做好後，就會被裝在餐桶送到學校。每一個餐桶裡都裝著相同的菜色，所以不必擔心餐桶擺放的位置會搞混。各班接著派代表到放置餐桶的地方，將食物抬回他們的教室。 在我們學校，餐桶放在靠近教室前方黑板的椅子或是桌子上。然後，志願幫忙盛飯菜的學生，便會把食物平均分配給每一位同學。如果分完後還有剩下的話，同學們就可以再取用一次飯菜。就如同您在第二張照片中所看到的，這群學生們都已是飢腸轆轆，迫不及待地要開動了!  每一個學生都會帶一個裝餐具和不鏽鋼碗的小餐袋。他們每天都帶餐袋來學校。不過要是他們忘了帶的話，學校裡也有免洗餐具，所以就用不著擔心會餓肚子了。吃完午餐後，每一位學生都要負責清洗他們自己的餐具。

閱讀理解 Questions

Do most schools in Taiwan have cafeterias?

No, most schools in Taiwan do not have cafeterias.

What is the food packed into so that it can be brought to the schools?

It is packed into containers.

What do the students have to pack every day so that they can eat?

They have to pack utensils and a bowl.

Unit 6: Jack Eats Out

⬇ PDF

Jack and his family are eating out this evening. The place they have chosen is a buffet-style restaurant. These restaurants offer a wide range of foods prepared in many ways.

After taking a plate to put the food on, Jack heads to the steam table. The steam table keeps the food warm even after sitting for some time. Each tray on the table contains a different dish. Jack eyes the tray heaped with stewed tofu. He thinks it looks very good, so he digs right in.

After putting a few vegetables on his plate, he takes the food to the cashier. In this restaurant you are charged by weight. This means you have to pay so much per gram of food. At some buffet-style restaurants you are charged by the number of items and not by weight, so if you want to sample many dishes it may be a lot more expensive.

Included with the price of Jack’s meal are soup and black tea. He takes advantage by repeatedly filling up his bowl with steaming hot soup. He sure loves soup, doesn't he?

Finally, Jack sits down to enjoy his meal. His family is still dishing up, but they will join him soon. By that time Jack will probably be ready for a second helping. Bon appetit, Jack!

課文朗讀（真人）
今天晚上，傑克和他的家人到外面去吃飯。他們所選擇的地點是一間自助式餐廳。這種餐廳提供了很多種以不同方式烹調的食物。 在拿了一個裝食物的盤子後，傑克走向了蒸氣保溫餐桌。這個餐桌可以保持食物的熱度，即使食物已經放了一段時間。桌上每個盤子都裝著不同的菜餚。傑克看著一個裝滿滷豆腐的托盤，他認為這道菜看起來很好吃，所以他立即挖了一勺。 在裝了一些青菜到他的餐盤之後，他端著盤子走到結帳處。這間餐廳是根據食物的重量來算錢的，這意謂著你必須按食物每公克的價格來付費。某些自助式餐廳是根據你吃幾種菜餚來算錢，而不是重量。所以，如果你想要品嚐很多樣的菜餚，那費用可能就會貴很多。 傑克這餐的費用包含了湯和紅茶。他善用這項優惠，將熱湯一碗一碗地盛到碗裡。他真的很喜歡喝湯，不是嗎? 最後，傑克坐下來享用他的餐點。他的家人們還在挑選菜餚，但他們很快就會加入他的行列。到那時候，傑克將已將準備好要吃第二盤了。傑克，祝你胃口大開!

Unit 7: Document Photos

⬇ PDF

In Taiwan there are a lot of forms that require photos to be attached. The standard size photo is 4 * 5 cm for most forms. They are especially important for passports, visas and hospital records. On such forms there is a box at the top specifically for this purpose.

Picture This girl is removing a photo from a small reddish envelope. She is filling out an application to take a GEPT test. The form requires her picture so that the test administrator can verify her identity when she takes the test. This prevents the girl from having another person take the test for her.

Picture 2 She applies glue to the back of the photo and then sticks it to the application. It is now ready to be mailed and, with any luck, she will be accepted to take the upcoming test; otherwise, she has to wait for the next test.

課文朗讀（真人）
在台灣有很多表格都要求貼上照片。在大多數的表格中，照片的標準尺寸為4*5公分。照片在護照、簽證及醫院記錄上格外重要。在這一類表格的上方會有一個小格子專門用來貼照片。照片一這女孩正從紅色小袋子裡拿出一張照片。她正在填寫參加全民英檢的報名表。這份表格需要貼上她的照片，以便監考人員在考試時能確認她的身份。這是為了防止她請別人代替她考試。照片二她在照片背後塗上膠水，然後把照片黏在報名表上。現在報名表準備要寄出去了。若一切順利，她的報名即將被受理，然後她便能參加最近的這次考試。不然，她就得等到下一梯次的測驗了。

Unit 8: Henry’s Vanity

⬇ PDF

Henry is quite fond of himself. He would stand in front of the mirror all day if his mother would allow it. He likes to try on many different outfits in his wardrobe and model them in front of the mirror, too. Sometimes, he even pretends he is on the catwalk modeling the latest fashions for all the designers to see.

Whatever his motivations are, his mother is disturbed by his vain performances and has removed all the mirrors and reflective surfaces from the house—even the toaster had to go. Now, if he wants to see himself, he has to fill the bathtub with water and stare at his reflection.

課文朗讀（真人）
亨利有點自戀。假如他的媽媽允許的話，他可能會一整天都站在鏡子前。他喜歡試穿衣櫥裡各種不同的服飾，然後在鏡子前展示一番。有時候，他甚至假裝自己正站在伸展台上，為所有的設計師展現最新流行的服飾。 不管他的動機為何，他如此愛慕虛榮的行徑，令他的媽媽困擾不已。她已經把家中所有表面可以反射的物品通通拿走，甚至連烤麵包機也一樣。現在，如果他想看到自己，他必須先將浴缸裝滿水，好凝視自己水中的倒影。

Unit 9: Bulletin Boards

⬇ PDF

Schools often use main entrances to post announcements for students and parents to read. This school is no exception as they have put up a number of bulletin boards along the main hallway. To prevent important notices from being removed or vandalized, they have covered the bulletin boards with protective sheets of glass.

Some things you may find on the announcement boards are class schedules, upcoming events, student awards, or notices to remind students of important test dates. Diligent students always check the board to make sure that they know what's going on at their school. The school staff is responsible for periodically updating the boards with current information and removing anything that is outdated, so that the information posted is always current and accurate.

課文朗讀（真人）
學校經常利用主要的入口張貼公告給學生和家長閱讀。這所學校也不例外，因為他們沿著川堂擺設一些佈告欄。為了防止重要的告示被移除或破壞，他們用防護的玻璃窗來遮住佈告欄。 一些你可以在佈告欄看到的訊息有班級課表、即將舉辦的活動、學生獎勵，或提醒學生重要考試日期的告示。用功的學生總會去查看佈告欄，以確認他們知道學校發生的事情。學校的職員負責定期更新佈告欄，他們張貼當前的資訊，並移除過期的資料，如此一來，張貼在上面的資訊就會一直保持是最新而且是正確的。

The school staff is responsible for updating the bulletin boards.

Unit 10: Bottled Water

⬇ PDF

When you are on the go, the best option to re-hydrate yourself is bottled water. It is readily available at most stores and there are countless brands and types of water to choose from. The number of choices is almost mind-boggling. You have distilled water, spring water, carbonated water and flavored water—just to name a few. Whatever their name, they all serve the same purpose: quenching your thirst.

The lady pictured here has just unscrewed the lid on her bottle of water. When buying bottled water, make sure the safety seal is not broken, so that you can be sure that the water hasn’t been tampered with and is fresh. If the seal is broken, it is best not to drink it just to be on the safe side.

In the second picture, our beautiful young model is taking a drink. She tips back the bottle and starts guzzling it down like there’s no tomorrow. She seems to be very thirsty. Perhaps she was lost in the desert and was unable to find water to quench her thirst. After finishing, she tosses the empty bottle into the recycling bin.

課文朗讀（真人）
當你外出時，補充水分最好的選擇就是瓶裝水。你可以輕易地在大部分商店買到瓶裝水，而且有非常多的品牌及種類可以買，可供選擇的數量多到幾乎令人吃驚。你可以選擇蒸餾水、礦泉水、汽水，及加味水等等，這只是其中的一些種類而已。不管它們的名稱是什麼，他們要達成的目的都是一樣的：解渴。 照片中的女士，剛剛把她的瓶裝水的瓶蓋打開。當購買瓶裝水時，要確定安全封口是完好無缺的，如此一來，你才有把握這瓶水沒有被蓄意毀損，而且是全新的。如果安全封口有破損，為了安全著想，最好不要去喝它。 在第二張圖當中，我們這位年輕貌美的模特兒正在喝水。她把瓶子拿斜並且開始狂飲，好像沒有明天似的。她似乎感到非常口渴。也許她在沙漠中迷路，無法找到水來解渴。喝完水後，她將空瓶丟到資源回收桶裡。
