Unit 1: Mailing My Friend

⬇ PDF

I haven’t seen my friend Kelly in a long time. She moved away to Pingdong three years ago because her father got a new job there. Since then, the only contact we’ve had is by mail. I really miss her.

我已經好久沒見到我的朋友凱莉了。三年前，因為她爸爸在屏東找到新工作，她就搬去那裡了。從那之後，我們唯一的聯絡方式就是寫信。我真的很想念她。

This week I wrote her a very long letter to catch up on everything that has been going on. As always, I took the letter down to the post office to be mailed. There are two mailboxes outside the main building to use. The red drop-off box has two slots, one for express mail and the other for airmail. I don’t use either because they cost more for postage than regular mail. The green mailbox is for regular mail and it also has two slots. The slot on the right is for local mail. The one on the left is for anything outside this township.

這星期我寫了一封很長的信給她，告訴她我這段時間發生的所有事情。一如往昔，我把信拿到郵局去寄。郵局主要建築物外面有兩個郵筒可以使用。紅色的投遞筒有兩個投遞口，一個是限時郵件專用，另一個是航空郵件專用。我兩個都不用，因為它們的郵資比普通信件貴。綠色郵筒是給普通信件用的，它也有兩個投遞口。右邊的投遞口是給本地信件用的；左邊的則是給寄到這個鄉鎮以外地方的信件用的。

Every time I drop a letter into the mailbox, I wonder how long it will be until my friend replies. Usually she writes back quickly, but if she is busy like I sometimes am, it can take a while. I hope it isn’t so long this time because I miss her so much.

每次我把信投進郵筒，我都會想知道要多久我朋友才會回信。通常她回信很快，但如果她像我有時候一樣忙碌，可能就要等上一段時間。我希望這次不會等太久，因為我真的很想念她。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

Why hasn’t the girl seen her friend in a long time?

Because her friend moved to Ping Dong.

What is the only contact the two girls have?

The only contact the two girls have is by mail.

How many mailboxes are there outside the post office?

There are two mailboxes there.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

contact(n.)聯絡；聯繫The only contact we have had since she moved is by letter.自從她搬走後，我們唯一的聯絡方式就是寫信。catch up on(ph.)得到...消息We met for coffee to catch up on everything that had happened.我們約喝咖啡，聊聊這段時間發生的所有事。go on(ph.)發生I wrote a long letter about everything that had been going on in my life.我寫了一封很長的信，講述我生活中發生的一切事情。as always(ph.)一如往昔As always, she mailed the letter from the post office near her house.一如往昔，她從家附近的郵局把信寄出去。mailbox(n.)郵筒There are two mailboxes outside the post office, one red and one green.郵局外面有兩個郵筒，一個是紅色的，一個是綠色的。main(adj.)主要的The mailboxes stand right in front of the main building.郵筒就立在主要建築物的前面。drop-off box(ph.)投遞筒The red drop-off box is used for express mail and airmail.紅色的投遞筒是用來投遞限時郵件和航空郵件的。slot(n.)(投放或插入東西的）狹縫Each mailbox has two slots for different kinds of mail.每個郵筒都有兩個狹縫，供不同種類的郵件投遞。express mail(ph.)限時郵件Sending a letter as express mail costs more, but it arrives faster.用限時郵件寄信雖然比較貴，但送達得比較快。airmail(n.)航空郵件If you want to send a letter overseas quickly, you can use airmail.如果你想快速把信寄到國外，可以使用航空郵件。postage(n.)郵資The postage for express mail is higher than for regular mail.限時郵件的郵資比普通信件還要高。regular mail(ph.)普通信件；平信Since I am not in a hurry, I always send my letters as regular mail.因為我不趕時間，所以我一直都是用平信寄信。local(adj.)當地的The right slot on the green mailbox is only for local mail.綠色郵筒右邊的狹縫只能投遞當地信件。township(n.)鄉；鎮Mail going outside this township must be dropped in the left slot.要寄到這個鄉鎮以外的信件，必須投進左邊的狹縫。drop(v.)投遞I always drop my letters into the green mailbox on my way to school.我上學途中總是把信投進那個綠色郵筒裡。wonder(v.)想知道Every time I mail a letter, I wonder how soon my friend will write back.每次寄信，我都會想知道我的朋友多快會回信。reply(v.)回信She usually replies to my letters within a week.她通常會在一週內回覆我的信。while(n.)一段時間If my friend is busy, it can take a while before she writes back.如果我朋友很忙，可能要過一段時間她才會回信。

Unit 2: Withdrawing Money

⬇ PDF

I recently needed to withdraw some money from my account at the post office. I went there with my passbook and chop. On my way in, I grabbed a withdrawal slip and found an empty spot at the counter.

最近我需要從我在郵局的帳戶裡領一些錢。我帶著存摺和印章去了那裡。在進去的路上，我拿了一張提領單，並在櫃檯找到一個空位。

I filled in the required information on the slip, such as the account number, my name and the amount that I wished to take out. I wrote neatly and carefully so that the teller would have no trouble reading it. Instead of using my signature, I used a chop. I lightly dabbed the stamp on the red inkpad and then pressed it onto the form.

我在單子上填寫了所需的資料，例如帳號、我的姓名，以及我想提領的金額。我寫得工整仔細，好讓出納員能輕鬆辨認。我沒有簽名，而是用了印章。我把印章輕輕地在紅色印台上沾了一下，然後把它蓋在表格上。

I must have stood in line for about five minutes, waiting to see the teller at the service counter. When it was finally my turn, I handed her my passbook and the withdrawal slip.

我大概排隊等了五分鐘，才輪到我在服務櫃檯與出納員辦理。終於輪到我時，我把存摺和提領單交給了她。

She smiled as she opened the passbook and asked me to key in my password. She then typed a few numbers into her computer and placed my passbook into the printer to be updated. Next, she counted out my money and returned my updated passbook to me.

她微笑著打開存摺，請我輸入密碼。接著她在電腦上打了幾個數字，並把我的存摺放進印表機裡更新。接下來，她點了我的錢，並把更新後的存摺還給我。

I thanked the teller for her help. Then I placed the money in my wallet. With the transaction completed, I happily walked out of the post office and went home.

我向出納員道謝，感謝她的幫忙。然後我把錢放進皮夾裡。交易完成後，我開心地走出郵局回家了。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

What did I take with me so that I could withdraw money?

You took your passbook and chop with you.

What did I use instead of a signature?

You used your chop.

Unit 3: Sore Eyes

⬇ PDF

Cara spent the morning walking around Tienwei for exercise. At the time, a lot of farmers were burning the stubble left over in the rice fields after the harvest. Thick black smoke hung in the morning air and made it a little difficult to breathe and after spending an extended period of time strolling around in the lingering haze, Cara’s eyes became dry, irritated, and bloodshot, so she returned home. Her eyes have been bothering her ever since.

卡菈整個早上都在田尾附近散步運動。當時，許多農夫正在焚燒收割後稻田裡留下的殘株。濃濃的黑煙瀰漫在清晨的空氣中，讓人有點難以呼吸，而在瀰漫不散的煙霧中散步了很長一段時間後，卡菈的眼睛變得乾澀、刺痛，並且佈滿血絲，於是她便回家了。從那時起，她的眼睛就一直讓她很不舒服。

Cara used some eye drops to soothe her sore eyes. First, she twisted the top off the small bottle and double-checked the directions to make sure she was using it properly—she didn’t want to take any chances with her eyesight. She then tilted her head back and carefully pulled her bottom eyelid open. She tried to keep from blinking by staring away from the bottle towards the roof.

卡菈用了一些眼藥水來緩解她痠痛的眼睛。首先，她轉開小瓶子的蓋子，並再三確認使用說明，以確保自己用法正確——她不想拿自己的視力冒任何風險。接著，她把頭向後仰，小心翼翼地把下眼瞼拉開。她試著盯著天花板、避開瓶子，好讓自己不要眨眼。

Gently, she squeezed the bottle and a drop of the liquid fell onto her bloodshot eye. It began to feel cool and immediately felt better, so she repeated the process with the other eye.

她輕輕擠壓瓶子，一滴液體落在她充血的眼睛上。那感覺立刻變得涼涼的，也馬上舒服許多，於是她用同樣的方式點了另一隻眼睛。

Although her eyes remained bloodshot and puffy for a couple of hours afterwards, by the next day they had completely recovered. The eye drops definitely helped to speed up the healing process. Without them, she might have had sore eyes for a few days.

雖然接下來幾個小時她的眼睛依然佈滿血絲又腫脹，但到了隔天，眼睛已經完全復原了。眼藥水確實有助於加速痊癒的過程。如果沒有使用眼藥水，她的眼睛痠痛可能會持續好幾天。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

What was going on in Tienwei while Cara was walking?

A lot of farmers were burning leftover stubble in the rice fields.

What did the smoke do to Cara’s eyes?

It made them dry, irritated and bloodshot.

What did she do to make sure she used the eye drops properly?

She double-checked the directions.

生字及片語 Words & Phrases

stubble(n.)(收割後餘留的)殘株The farmer set fire to the dry stubble left in the field after the harvest.農夫將收成後田裡留下的乾燥殘株點火燒掉。harvest(n.)收成This year's rice harvest was much larger than expected.今年的稻米收成比預期的多很多。extended(adj.)長期的After an extended walk in the smoky air, her eyes started to hurt.在煙霧瀰漫的空氣中走了很長一段時間後，她的眼睛開始感到不舒服。lingering(adj.)逗留不去的The lingering smoke made it hard to breathe long after the fire was out.即使火已經熄滅，逗留不去的煙霧仍讓人難以呼吸。haze(n.)薄霧；煙霧A thick haze covered the fields all morning.一整個早上，田野都籠罩在濃濃的煙霧中。irritated(adj.)過敏的；疼痛的Her skin felt irritated after walking through the dusty smoke.走過滿是塵土的煙霧後，她的皮膚感到刺痛。bloodshot(adj.)充滿血絲的His eyes turned bloodshot from staying up all night.他熬夜一整晚後，眼睛都充滿了血絲。bother(v.)煩擾A sore throat continued to bother her for the rest of the day.喉嚨痛的問題持續困擾她一整天。eye drop(ph.)眼藥水She used an eye drop to relieve her dry, itchy eyes.她用眼藥水來緩解乾澀發癢的眼睛。soothe(v.)緩和；減輕Cool water can help soothe irritated skin.冷水有助於舒緩過敏的皮膚。direction(n.)指示；用法說明Always read the directions carefully before taking any medicine.服藥前一定要仔細閱讀用法說明。properly(adv.)正確地；適當地Make sure you store the medicine properly so it does not go bad.請確保正確地保存藥品，以免變質。eyelid(n.)眼瞼She gently pulled down her lower eyelid to apply the drop.她輕輕拉下下眼瞼來點眼藥水。blink(v.)眨眼睛Try not to blink while the drop falls into your eye.當眼藥水滴進眼睛時，盡量不要眨眼。liquid(n.)液體A clear liquid dripped slowly from the bottle.一滴透明的液體從瓶子裡緩緩滴落。remain(v.)維持Her eyes will remain a little red for a few hours after using the drops.使用眼藥水後，她的眼睛會有幾個小時維持些微紅腫。puffy(adj.)腫脹的His eyes looked puffy after crying.他哭過之後，眼睛看起來腫腫的。recover(v.)復原It usually takes a day or two to recover from sore, irritated eyes.眼睛痠痛過敏通常需要一兩天才能復原。definitely(adv.)明顯地；肯定地Eye drops definitely helped her eyes feel better faster.眼藥水確實明顯地讓她的眼睛更快好轉。healing(adj.)康復的Resting well can speed up the healing process after an injury.充分休息能加快受傷後的康復過程。

Unit 4: At the Gas Station

⬇ PDF

Picture 1 A scooter has just pulled into the gas station to fuel up. The attendant asks the customer what she wants. The customer asks her to fill it up with 95 octane fuel. The attendant then grabs the nozzle and turns the pump on.

圖片1 一輛摩托車剛駛進加油站要加油。服務員詢問顧客想要什麼。顧客請她加滿95辛烷值的汽油。服務員接著抓起加油槍噴嘴，把幫浦打開。

Picture 2 The customer opens the lid that covers the fuel cap. Then she twists off the cap. She twists very hard because the cap is on tight.

圖片2 顧客打開蓋住油箱蓋的蓋子，然後擰開油箱蓋。她用力擰，因為蓋子鎖得很緊。

Picture 3 The attendant inserts the nozzle and pulls the trigger. Gas begins to fill the tank and the smelly, flammable fumes rise into the air. She is careful not to pump it out too fast or it might splash out onto the ground.

圖片3 服務員把噴嘴插入油箱，按下啟動裝置。汽油開始注入油箱，難聞又易燃的氣體也隨之飄散到空氣中。她小心不要加得太快，以免汽油濺到地上。

Picture 4 So that it is easier to make change, the attendant tries to round off the price to a multiple of ten. After finishing, she hangs the nozzle back on the pump and the patron hands her some money. She leaves to put the money in the till and returns with some change and a receipt. The satisfied customer then takes off with a full tank of gas.

圖片4 為了方便找零，服務員試著把價錢湊成十的倍數。加完油後，她把噴嘴掛回幫浦上，顧客遞給她一些錢。她離開去把錢放進錢櫃，然後拿著找零和收據回來。滿意的顧客隨後帶著加滿的油箱離去。
課文朗讀（真人）

閱讀理解 Questions

What kind of fuel does the customer want?

The customer wants 95 octane fuel.

What does the customer have to do before the attendant can fill up the scooter?

The customer has to open the lid that covers the fuel cap and twist off the cap.

Where does she hang up the nozzle when she is finished with it?

Unit 5: Crossing Guards

⬇ PDF

With so much traffic on the streets of Taiwan, safety becomes an issue whenever children are going home after school. Because of this, schools have appointed some students to be crossing guards. This is to make it a lot safer for everyone.

由於台灣街道上的交通量很大，每當孩子們放學回家時，安全就成了一個問題。因此，學校指派了一些學生擔任導護人員，讓大家都能更安全一些。

The idea is to have the crossing guards stop the oncoming traffic to let cyclists and walkers cross the streets safely. You can tell who the crossing guards are because they usually wear reflective orange vests and may carry a red flag. For added protection, some of them wear helmets in addition to the safety vests.

這個構想是讓導護人員攔下來車，好讓騎自行車和步行的人能安全地穿越馬路。你可以從他們身上分辨出誰是導護人員，因為他們通常穿著反光的橘色背心，並可能拿著一面紅旗。為了加強保護，有些人除了穿安全背心之外，還會戴上安全帽。

The red flag tells students that it is not safe to proceed. All the students then line up behind it and wait. When it is safe, the crossing guard will raise the flag and blow a whistle. Students are then permitted to go.

紅旗告訴學生們現在還不能通行。所有學生便在旗子後方排隊等候。等到安全的時候，導護人員會舉起旗子並吹哨子，學生們這時才被允許通行。

A long pole with brightly colored flags attached to it is used to stop cars. It is very long and can be held across an entire lane of traffic. When the pole is held horizontally across the lane, motorists must stop and wait until the crossing guards let all the students pass. Once it is clear, they will raise the pole vertically again.

一根綁著鮮豔旗子的長桿被用來攔停車輛。它非常長，可以橫跨整條車道。當這根桿子被水平舉起橫擋在車道上時，駕駛人必須停車，等待導護人員讓所有學生通過。等到路況淨空後，他們才會再將桿子垂直豎起。

The flashing light and pylons, or traffic cones, indicate where the cyclists can ride and they also make the crossing area more visible to motorists. It is another way to make the area safer.

閃爍的燈光和交通錐（也就是路標）標示出自行車可以行駛的範圍，也讓穿越區對駕駛人來說更加顯眼，這是讓這個區域更安全的另一種方法。

If you are driving in an area such as this, make sure you slow down and watch out for students crossing the road. Stop if the crossing guard indicates that you should do so. This will make it safer for everyone, especially the children.

如果你正在這樣的區域開車，請務必減速慢行，並留意正在過馬路的學生。如果導護人員示意你該停車，就請停下來。這樣做能讓大家都更安全，尤其是孩子們。

傳統上，舞獅是單獅的表演形式，但多年來在某些地區已經有了一些改變。有時候，較小的單人獅子會陪伴著那隻大獅子一起表演。這樣做或許能為表演增添一些興奮感，幫助娛樂孩子們。

Unit 8: Puffed Rice

⬇ PDF

Somewhere in Yuanlin on the side of the street, you may see this lady making puffed rice squares. To make such squares, rice, wheat or other grains are put into a pressure cooker (the old black thing with a wheel on the left side). The contents are then heated, causing the air pressure inside the cooker to increase dramatically. When the air pressure reaches a certain point, the contents pop—much the same way as popcorn does. Once the grain is popped, it is mixed with other ingredients to make it sweet and sticky. Then it is pressed into a wooden mold and cut into squares.

在員林街上的某個地方，你可能會看到這位女士在製作爆米香方塊。要做出這樣的方塊，米、小麥或其他穀類會先被放進壓力鍋（就是左側有個輪子的那台舊黑鍋）。接著內容物會被加熱，使鍋子內部的空氣壓力急遽上升。當空氣壓力達到某一個程度時，內容物就會爆開──就跟爆米花的原理一樣。穀粒爆開之後，會與其他成份混合，讓它變得香甜又黏稠。然後把它壓進木製模子裡，再切成一塊塊方塊。

The lady, shown here, is cutting the squares with a sharp knife. She uses a flat piece of wood as a guide to make her cuts nice and straight. To her left you can see the finished squares, bagged and ready for sale. They look scrumptious!

圖中這位女士正在用鋒利的刀切方塊。她用一塊平整的木片當導引，讓切線平整又筆直。在她左邊可以看到已經切好的方塊，裝袋準備出售。看起來真是美味極了！

Unit 9: Whack-A-Mole

⬇ PDF

Quentin is a very angry 14 year old boy. He is constantly in the principal’s office at school for starting fights in the hallway and for disobeying his teacher. He has so much trouble controlling his temper in fact, that his parents have even taken him to a psychologist. But to no avail.

昆丁是個十四歲、脾氣非常暴躁的男孩。他經常因為在走廊上和同學打架，以及不服從老師的指示，而被叫到校長室。他控制脾氣的能力實在太差了，父母甚至帶他去看心理醫生。但完全沒有用。

There is one thing, however, that helps Quentin to release his anger in a less violent way. It is a game called Whack-a-Mole.

然而，有一件事能幫助昆丁以比較不暴力的方式發洩他的怒氣，那就是一款叫做「打地鼠」的遊戲。

Whack-a-Mole is an arcade type game. The object is to hit the mole as it comes out of its hole. The more moles you hit, or “whack”, the more points you get and the longer you can play, but it isn’t as easy as you think.

打地鼠是一種電玩遊樂場的遊戲。遊戲的目標是在地鼠從洞裡冒出來的時候打中牠。你打中（或說「打擊」）的地鼠越多，得到的分數就越高，能玩的時間也越長，但這其實沒有你想的那麼簡單。

The moles pop up and down very quickly. There are six different holes, too and you never know which hole a mole is going to come out of. Plus, the longer the game goes on, the faster the moles go up and down. You definitely need good hand-eye coordination to play.

地鼠會非常快速地冒出來又縮回去。而且總共有六個不同的洞，你永遠不知道地鼠會從哪一個洞裡冒出來。此外，遊戲進行得越久，地鼠冒出來又縮回去的速度就越快。要玩這個遊戲，你絕對需要良好的手眼協調能力。

When Quentin plays the game, he releases a lot of pent-up energy. After he is finished, he is always very calm. Perhaps his school should consider putting a Whack-a-Mole machine in his classroom. If nothing else, it would prevent him from playing Whack-a-Classmate or, worse yet, Whack-a-Teacher.

當昆丁玩這個遊戲時，他釋放了大量被壓抑的精力。玩完之後，他總是變得非常平靜。或許他的學校應該考慮在他的教室裡放一台打地鼠機台。如果沒有別的好處，至少能防止他去玩「打同學」，或者更糟的，「打老師」。
課文朗讀（真人）

Unit 10: Using the Printer

⬇ PDF

Jenny needs to print off a few pictures for a social studies project. She is almost ready to start printing when she notices that the printer is out of paper. Luckily, she had just bought a package of paper the day before at an office supply store.

珍妮需要為社會科的報告印出幾張圖片。她正準備開始列印時，卻發現印表機沒紙了。幸好，她前一天才剛在辦公用品店買了一包紙。

She digs the package of paper out from under the clutter in her backpack and removes the wrapping. Not wanting the paper to get wrinkled or torn, she slowly removes a half a dozen sheets from the pack.

她從背包裡雜亂的東西中翻出那包紙，拆開包裝。為了不讓紙張弄皺或撕破，她慢慢地從那疊紙中抽出六張左右。

Next Jenny lifts the flap on the front of the printer and carefully feeds the paper into the tray until it won’t go any further. Then she closes the lid again and returns to her seat at the workstation.

接著珍妮掀起印表機前方的蓋子，小心地把紙放進紙匣，一直放到不能再放為止。然後她蓋上蓋子，回到工作站的座位上。

The pictures she wants to print are already on the desktop and ready to go. She hits the print button in the picture viewer window and the printer menu pops up. Here she selects the number of copies she wants and clicks on the okay button.

她要列印的圖片早已顯示在桌面上，隨時可以列印。她在圖片檢視器視窗裡按下列印鍵，印表機的選單就跳了出來。她在這裡選好要印的份數，然後按下確定鍵。

The printer begins to hum and the ink cartridges dance back and forth inside the printer as it prepares to print. A single sheet of paper begins to disappear inside the machine. Once again, the ink cartridges are moving, this time printing a picture.

印表機開始發出嗡嗡聲，墨水匣在機器裡來回移動，準備開始列印。一張紙開始消失進機器裡。墨水匣再次移動，這次是在印一張圖片。

In no time at all, the paper can be seen re-entering the outside world, but now it is full of color. Picture after picture are printed off and drop onto the table. As the last of the pictures is finished, the printer returns to standby mode. Jenny shuffles through the papers to make sure they are all there and then dashes off to class.

沒多久，那張紙又從機器裡重新出現，但現在上面已經印滿了色彩。一張又一張的圖片被印出來，掉落在桌上。當最後一張圖片印完後，印表機便進入待機模式。珍妮翻看這些照片，確認全部都在，然後便衝去上課了。
