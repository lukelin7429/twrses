Unit 1: Pretending

⬇ PDF

It is a rainy day in October. Joe and Steven are terribly bored. They need a way to keep occupied without getting into too much trouble.

Joe has an idea. He is going to use his father's new digital camera to take pictures of Steven while Steven pretends to be a motorcycle racer.

Joe's little brother's bicycle makes a perfect make-believe motorbike. Steven hops on. He burns around the living room pretending that he is in a race. Joe acts like a photo journalist and begins snapping pictures.

Soon Steven approaches the finish line and raises his hand in victory. Joe tries to capture this glorious moment on his camera for the front page of tomorrow's sports section.

Steven and Joe continue pretending like this for a few more minutes, but soon they grow tired of this game and boredom sets in again. They move on, looking for something more exciting to do. Hopefully, they don’t find any mischief on their way.

課文朗讀
十月的一個雨天，喬和史蒂文非常無聊。他們需要找個方法讓自己有事可做，又不要惹上太多麻煩。喬想到一個主意。他要用爸爸的新數位相機，拍史蒂文假裝成摩托車賽車手的照片。喬弟弟的腳踏車正好可以當作想像中的摩托車。史蒂文跳上車，在客廳裡到處衝，假裝自己正在比賽。喬則裝成攝影記者，開始拍照。很快地，史蒂文接近終點線，並舉手慶祝勝利。喬想用相機捕捉這個光榮時刻，放到明天體育版的頭版。史蒂文和喬又這樣假裝玩了幾分鐘，但很快就玩膩了，無聊又回來了。他們繼續尋找更刺激的事來做。希望他們一路上不要找到什麼惡作劇。

Unit 2: Cutting the Lawn

⬇ PDF

The lawn. It seems like you just finish cutting it and it is time to do it again. It grows so quickly that it is a never ending struggle to keep it short.

Here my mother is pushing the lawn mower alongside a green fence. She is sheltered briefly by the shade of the bushes behind the fence, but soon she will have to cut the grass in the middle of the yard out in the hot sun. She will have to be careful that she doesn't hit her head on the bird feeder that hangs from the lilacs. She wears gloves so that she doesn’t get blisters on her hands. It is also a good idea to wear shoes because sometimes rocks and other hard things fly out from under the mower and hit your feet.

Now my father is helping my mother to empty the mower bag. The mower bag collects the grass clippings so that you don’t have to spend extra time raking. If the lawn is really long, the bag will fill up quickly, but usually you can mow for quite a while before the bag needs emptying. The grass clippings are put into a garbage bag and then they can be composted or used as mulch for the garden.

課文朗讀
草坪。感覺才剛割完，又到了要再割的時候。草長得非常快，所以要讓它保持短短的，像是一場永無止境的奮戰。這裡，我媽媽正沿著綠色圍籬推著割草機。她暫時受到圍籬後方灌木陰影的遮蔽，但很快就必須到院子中央，在炎熱的太陽下割草。她還得小心不要撞到掛在紫丁香上的鳥類餵食器。她戴著手套，這樣手上才不會起水泡。穿鞋也是個好主意，因為有時石頭和其他堅硬的東西會從割草機底下飛出來，打到你的腳。現在我爸爸正在幫媽媽清空割草機的集草袋。集草袋會收集草屑，這樣你就不用另外花時間耙草。如果草坪很長，袋子很快就會裝滿；但通常可以割上一段時間才需要清空。草屑會被放進垃圾袋，之後可以做成堆肥，或當作花園的護根物。

Unit 3: Digging a Drainage Ditch

⬇ PDF

Putting in a drainage ditch by hand is a lot of work, but not if you know someone with a backhoe. With a backhoe, it is relatively easy to dig large holes as its long powerful arm is able to dig into the ground with ease.

At the foot of these steps, this hoe is digging a long, narrow ditch. However, before the hoe operator was able to begin digging, someone had to break up the concrete with a jackhammer. After that, the backhoe could start.

The hoe operator sits on the large machine and uses the controls to move the machine around. He is very careful while working so that he doesn't do any more damage than is necessary. He also has to watch carefully for underground lines and pipes.

To dig a hole, the operator extends the boom and opens the bucket. When the bucket touches the ground, he slowly closes it and moves the arm toward the hoe. This process fills the bucket with dirt. The arm is then raised as the machine turns. When the bucket is over the place where he wants to make a pile, he opens it again and empties the dirt. All this is done without actually moving anywhere, because the body of the machine rotates on its tracks. This process is repeated until you have a big hole or, in this case, a ditch.

課文朗讀
用手挖排水溝是非常辛苦的工作，但如果你認識有反鏟挖土機的人，就不一樣了。有了反鏟挖土機，挖大洞相對容易，因為它又長又有力的手臂能輕鬆挖入地面。在這些台階底下，這台挖土機正在挖一條又長又窄的溝。不過，在操作員開始挖之前，必須先有人用破碎機把水泥打碎。之後，反鏟挖土機才能開始工作。操作員坐在大型機器上，用控制桿移動機器。他工作時非常小心，以免造成不必要的損壞。他也必須仔細注意地下的線路和管線。要挖洞時，操作員伸出吊臂並打開挖斗。當挖斗碰到地面時，他慢慢關起挖斗，並把手臂往機器方向移動。這個過程會讓挖斗裝滿泥土。接著手臂升起，機器轉動。當挖斗到達他想堆土的地方上方時，他再次打開挖斗，把泥土倒出來。這一切都可以在機器本身不移動位置的情況下完成，因為機身會在履帶上旋轉。這個過程會一直重複，直到挖出一個大洞，或像這次一樣，挖出一條溝。

Unit 4: Oral Presentations

⬇ PDF

In today’s working world, we often need to communicate ideas to large audiences. This is usually done through oral presentations. Thus, mastering the art of the oral presentation is an important skill that can help one be successful in his or her profession.

Audio and visual aids are two things that can be incorporated into an oral presentation. They are both great ways to enhance your presentation and make sure the audience clearly understands your main idea. For example, PC projectors can be used to show simple visual aids like graphs, charts or pictures on your topic. Try and keep it simple, though. Inundate your audience with too much information and you might confuse them.

As the speaker, your words and ideas are what everyone comes to hear about. The presentation should only aid you and not be the primary focus; it shouldn’t be ALL about flashy computer images, and super detailed charts, even though they may help. If people leave your presentation remembering nothing but a bunch of graphs or leave blinded from staring at a white projector screen, chances are you didn't give a successful oral presentation.

課文朗讀
在今日的職場中，我們常常需要向大量聽眾傳達想法。這通常是透過口頭報告來完成。因此，掌握口頭報告的技巧是一項重要能力，能幫助人在自己的職業中成功。音訊和視覺輔助是可以加入口頭報告的兩種工具。它們都能提升你的報告，並確保聽眾清楚理解你的主要想法。例如，電腦投影機可以用來展示簡單的視覺輔助，如與主題相關的圖表、統計圖或圖片。不過，要盡量保持簡單。若向聽眾灌輸太多資訊，可能會讓他們困惑。身為講者，大家是來聽你的話語和想法。簡報應該只是輔助你，而不是主要焦點；即使華麗的電腦影像和超詳細圖表可能有幫助，報告也不應該全都圍繞著它們。如果人們離開你的報告時，只記得一堆圖表，或因盯著白色投影幕而眼花，那你很可能沒有做出成功的口頭報告。

閱讀理解 Questions

Unit 5: Sheet Protectors

⬇ PDF

Whether you are an elementary school student or a doctor, you must take good care of some of your important papers. Keeping such papers in sheet protectors is a great way to both organize and protect them. Without them, your materials may become torn (ripped), wrinkled or dirty.

Look at Colby. He is preparing a presentation for his social studies class.

He has a binder, in which he has organized all the papers that he needs. Each paper has been carefully placed into a sheet protector. He leafs through his work to make sure everything is in order. It is a good thing that he has done this because a couple of the pages weren’t in order. He removes them from the sheet protector and reinserts them in their correct places.

Now Colby is ready for his presentation. He is confident that he will do well as he has learned that using sheet protectors will not only give his project a professional look, but more importantly, he knows that his papers will not become unorganized or get damaged.

So learn from Colby: If you want to keep your school work neat and tidy, go out and stock up on sheet protectors today.

課文朗讀
無論你是小學生還是醫生，你都必須好好照顧一些重要文件。把這些文件放在文件保護套裡，是整理並保護它們的好方法。沒有保護套，你的資料可能會被撕破、起皺或弄髒。看看科爾比。他正在為社會課準備報告。他有一本活頁夾，裡面整理了所有需要的文件。每一張紙都被仔細放進文件保護套中。他翻閱自己的資料，確認一切都按順序排列。他這樣做真是好事，因為有幾頁順序不對。他把它們從保護套中取出，再重新放回正確的位置。現在科爾比已經準備好報告了。他很有信心自己會表現得很好，因為他學到使用文件保護套不只會讓他的作業看起來專業，更重要的是，他知道自己的文件不會變得亂七八糟或受到損壞。所以向科爾比學習吧：如果你想讓學校作業保持整潔，今天就去多買一些文件保護套吧。

Unit 6: Staring Death in the Face

⬇ PDF

Somewhere in Taiwan a daring, and very handsome man taunts death by forcing open the mouth of a ferocious lion. Moments earlier, the man had been waiting for a call. Not knowing the beast was still alive, he got a little too close. When his cell-phone rang the lion mistook it for a tasty bird and swallowed it whole.

Apparently, the call the man was expecting was very important; important enough to risk his life for. He wrestled the lion’s jaws apart and carefully moved his arms past the razor-sharp teeth, over the slimy tongue and into the lion's throat. A small crowd that had gathered cheered as he removed his arm and held aloft his saliva and slime covered phone.

Surely, such a display of bravery and phenomenal strength has never been seen by mortal eyes before. Nor will it probably ever be seen again.

課文朗讀
在台灣某處，一位大膽而且非常英俊的男子，強行打開一隻凶猛獅子的嘴，挑戰死亡。就在不久前，這名男子一直在等一通電話。他不知道這隻野獸還活著，所以靠得太近。當他的手機響起時，獅子把它誤認為一隻美味的小鳥，整個吞了下去。顯然，男子等待的那通電話非常重要，重要到值得冒生命危險。他用力扳開獅子的下顎，小心地把手臂伸過鋒利如剃刀的牙齒、黏滑的舌頭，進入獅子的喉嚨。一小群聚集起來的人，在他抽出手臂並高高舉起沾滿口水和黏液的手機時歡呼。毫無疑問，凡人的眼睛從未見過如此勇敢與驚人力量的展現，恐怕以後也不會再見到了。

Unit 7: You scratch my back and I’ll eat whatever is crawling on yours

⬇ PDF

Away from the troop and sitting high above the forest floor, these macaques take turns grooming one another. They search through each other's fur, trying to find ticks, lice, and anything crawly that shouldn't be there.

Grooming has two very important benefits. Firstly, it is quality social time, not unlike the time people spend together talking over coffee. The grooming helps to strengthen bonds between individuals and in turn this helps these very social animals maintain a sense of community.

Secondly, grooming is very important for the health of these macaques. A friend can remove nasty ticks and biting insects which they wouldn't normally be able to reach by themselves. Too many parasites on one monkey can lower the immune system and even cause disease and infection.

But what does the groomer get out of all of this hard work? What’s in it for him? Well, it seems, this habitual grooming does come with a reward of sorts after all; A big juicy one, in fact. You see, each parasite removed becomes a tasty snack for the grooming macaque to enjoy!

課文朗讀
遠離猴群、坐在森林地面上方高處的這些獼猴，輪流為彼此理毛。牠們在彼此的毛髮中搜尋，試著找出蜱蟲、蝨子，以及任何不該在那裡爬來爬去的東西。理毛有兩個非常重要的好處。第一，這是有品質的社交時間，就像人們一起喝咖啡聊天一樣。理毛能幫助個體之間加強連結，進而幫助這些非常社會化的動物維持群體意識。第二，理毛對這些獼猴的健康非常重要。朋友可以幫忙除去牠們自己平常碰不到的討厭蜱蟲和會咬人的昆蟲。一隻猴子身上的寄生蟲太多，可能會降低免疫系統，甚至造成疾病和感染。不過，替別人理毛的猴子從這些辛苦工作中得到什麼呢？牠有什麼好處？嗯，看來這種習慣性的理毛終究還是有某種獎賞，而且其實是又大又多汁的獎賞。你看，每一隻被除下來的寄生蟲，都會變成理毛獼猴可以享用的美味點心！

Unit 8: Sculpting

⬇ PDF

What may appear to be an ordinary block of wood to you, is much, much more to these men. They are sculptors and, to them, a block of wood is a work of art waiting to be revealed to the world.

In order to make their visions a reality, these sculptors will use a variety of tools. In the picture on the left, the sculptor uses a mallet and chisel to carve a beautiful fish dancing on the water's surface out of a block of wood. With these tools, he gradually chips away at the wood, slowly revealing the hidden beauty within.

In the picture on the right, the sculptor is working on a depiction of a big-horned sheep. His detailed work shows a ram climbing up a mountainous, rocky cliff. For some of the more intricate details he uses a pneumatic (air) rotary-tool, which makes working in hard to reach areas much easier. Notice the orange airline that feeds air to the tool.

Starting out as nothing more than plain looking chunks of wood, these sculptors have removed the outer layers, revealing truly amazing works of art within. Perhaps you will you never look at a block a wood in the same way again! Maybe you, too, will see the hidden beauty that beckons to be feed.

課文朗讀
對你來說看起來只是普通木塊的東西，對這些人而言卻有更多更多的意義。他們是雕刻家，對他們來說，一塊木頭就是等待向世界展現的藝術品。為了讓他們的想像成真，這些雕刻家會使用各式各樣的工具。在左邊的圖片中，雕刻家用木槌和鑿子，從一塊木頭中雕出一條美麗的魚，像是在水面上舞動。透過這些工具，他逐漸鑿掉木頭，一點一點顯露其中隱藏的美。在右邊的圖片中，雕刻家正在製作一件大角羊的作品。他精細的作品呈現一隻公羊攀爬在多山、多岩石的峭壁上。為了處理一些更複雜精細的細節，他使用氣動旋轉工具，這讓在難以觸及的地方工作容易許多。注意那條橘色的空氣管，它把空氣送到工具中。這些作品一開始不過是看起來平凡的木塊，但雕刻家去除了外層，展現出其中真正令人驚嘆的藝術品。也許你再也不會用同樣的眼光看待一塊木頭了！也許你也會看見那呼喚著要被釋放的隱藏之美。

Unit 9: Gondolas

⬇ PDF

Trucks, cars and buses are not always the most practical way to transport people from point A to point B. This is particularly true when point B is hundreds of meters up the side of a steep mountain. In such cases gondolas are much better.

Gondolas use heavy cables strung between poles to carry cars up and down mountains. Usually they run high above the tree line, giving you a bird's-eye-view of the surroundings.

In the picture on the left, we can see passengers who have purchased tickets and are waiting to board the gondola running between Taipei Zoo and Maokong. After a long wait in line, the passengers are excited as they are finally preparing to board the gondola. The doors of the car open automatically. Once on, the doors close again and the car swings into motion.

As you exit the terminal, the floor quickly drops away as does your stomach. Now all that is below you are trees and plenty of air as you climb steadily, onward and upward. It is very picturesque. You can also see other cars suspended in front of you.

From this viewpoint, the world seems quite amazing. It is a rare opportunity to experience things dangling from such heights. In reality, our world is very two dimensional (other than climbing a flight of stares). The vertical is something that maybe only pilots and birds really navigate. If only for a moment, taking a ride on a gondola can give us a new perspective on the world around us.

課文朗讀
卡車、汽車和公車並不總是把人從 A 點運送到 B 點最實用的方法。當 B 點位在陡峭山坡上數百公尺高的地方時，這一點尤其明顯。在這種情況下，纜車就好得多。纜車使用架在柱子之間的粗重纜線，載著車廂上下山。通常它們會行駛在樹梢線上方，讓你能鳥瞰周遭景色。在左邊的圖片中，我們可以看到已買票、正在等待搭乘台北動物園到貓空之間纜車的乘客。排了很久的隊後，乘客終於準備上纜車，感到很興奮。車廂門會自動打開。一上車後，門又關上，車廂開始擺動前進。當你離開車站時，腳下的地面迅速遠離，你的胃也像跟著往下掉。現在你下方只剩樹木和大量空氣，而你穩定地向前、向上攀升。景色非常如畫。你也可以看見其他車廂懸掛在前方。從這個視角看，世界似乎相當奇妙。從這麼高的地方懸在空中體驗事物，是難得的機會。事實上，我們的世界非常平面化（除了爬一段樓梯之外）。垂直方向或許只有飛行員和鳥類真正能自在穿梭。即使只有片刻，搭乘纜車也能讓我們用新的視角看待周遭的世界。

Unit 10: Jack-o'-lanterns

⬇ PDF

Look at all the scary faces on the pumpkins. It must be Halloween. Making jack-o'-lanterns is a traditional activity this time of year. People use them to decorate their windows and front porches so that their house looks spooky.

Making a jack-o'-lantern is quite simple and fun. All that is needed is a knife, a spoon and a candle. Start by cutting a lid out of the top. Cut it at an angle so that the lid will sit on the top and not fall in. Next, use your hands or a spoon to scoop out the seeds and guts of the pumpkin. When the inside is clean, you are ready to carve the face.

Before cutting the face, first draw it on with a marker. This makes it easier to cut as you can just follow the lines. Be careful with the knife so that you don't cut yourself.

The final step is to place a candle inside the hollowed out pumpkin. When the candle is lit, the eerie face will glow in the dark, casting an evil light on everything around. Place the jack-o'-lantern in a safe place where nothing will catch on fire. Also, blow the candle out before you go to bed or leave the house.

When Halloween is over, don't forget to throw the pumpkin away or it will rot, stink and attract flies. Or you could make pumpkin soup for your family and friends.

課文朗讀
看看南瓜上那些嚇人的臉。一定是萬聖節到了。製作南瓜燈是每年這個時候的傳統活動。人們用它們裝飾窗戶和前門廊，讓房子看起來陰森可怕。製作南瓜燈其實很簡單，也很有趣。只需要一把刀、一支湯匙和一根蠟燭。先從頂端切出一個蓋子。切的時候要有角度，這樣蓋子才會坐在上方，不會掉進去。接著，用手或湯匙把南瓜籽和裡面的東西挖出來。當內部清乾淨後，就可以開始雕刻臉了。在切臉之前，先用麥克筆畫上去。這樣會比較容易切，因為你只要沿著線切就好。使用刀子時要小心，免得割傷自己。最後一步是把蠟燭放進挖空的南瓜裡。蠟燭點燃時，詭異的臉會在黑暗中發光，把邪惡的光投射到周圍的一切。把南瓜燈放在安全的地方，確保沒有東西會著火。另外，睡覺或離開家之前要把蠟燭吹熄。萬聖節結束後，別忘了把南瓜丟掉，否則它會腐爛、發臭並吸引蒼蠅。或者你也可以為家人和朋友做南瓜湯。
